Overview

Usage Guidelines

Do's ‚úÖ

  • Use bold, high-contrast designs
  • Incorporate diagonal stripe patterns
  • Use large, uppercase headings
  • Add industrial textures (metal, concrete)
  • Use solid, offset shadows for depth
  • Embrace asymmetry and strong visual weight

Don'ts ‚ùå

  • Don't use soft, rounded shapes
  • Don't use pastel or muted colors
  • Don't create delicate, thin designs
  • Don't overuse gradients (solid colors preferred)
  • Don't use script or decorative fonts
  • Don't create overly symmetrical layouts

Why Choose This Option?

Best For

  • Heavy equipment financing (construction, mining, forestry)
  • Male-dominated industries (75%+ male decision-makers)
  • Companies wanting to emphasize strength and reliability
  • Markets where "heavy-duty" is a key value proposition
  • Brands wanting to stand out with bold, unapologetic design

Considerations

  • Very masculine aesthetic (may not appeal to all industries)
  • Bold colors may overwhelm if overused
  • Less suitable for light manufacturing or food service equipment
  • May feel "too aggressive" for some clients
