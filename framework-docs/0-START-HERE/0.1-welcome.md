## The D/A/F Workflow (Your Only Job)

You'll hear "D/A/F" mentioned constantly. Here's what it means:

**D/ = Draft (AI just made this)**
- AI generates content
- Saves to D/ folder (Draft)
- Status: Waiting for your review

**A/ = Approved (You said it's good)**
- You reviewed the draft
- Quality is acceptable (70%+ score)
- Move file from D/ to A/ (Approved)
- Ready to publish

**F/ = Failed (You said it's bad)**
- You reviewed the draft
- Quality is below standard (<70% score)
- Move file from D/ to F/ (Failed)
- Archive with notes on why

**Your role:** Quality control inspector at a factory.

**Toys (content) come off the assembly line.**
- Good toys → Showroom (A/ folder → Published)
- Bad toys → Reject bin (F/ folder → Archived)

You don't make the toys (AI does that).

You just inspect them (5% of work).
