## Module 1: What Is This? (The Big Picture)

### Lesson 1.1: Introduction to SEO (1 hour)

**Learning Objective:**
Students will be able to explain what SEO is using real-world analogies.

**Materials Needed:**
- Computer with internet access
- Framework files accessible
- Whiteboard or digital drawing tool

**Lesson Plan:**

**Part 1: The Restaurant Analogy (20 minutes)**

**Activity: Draw Your Restaurant**
1. Give each student paper and markers
2. Ask: "Draw your dream restaurant"
3. Share drawings (5 min)
4. Explain: "Now imagine Google is GrabFood/Uber Eats"

**Discussion Questions:**
- How do customers find restaurants on delivery apps?
- What makes a restaurant show up first?
- How is this similar to websites on Google?

**Key Takeaways:**
- Good location = Good keywords
- Good reviews = Backlinks
- Good menu = Good content
- Clean kitchen = Technical SEO

**Part 2: What This Framework Does (20 minutes)**

**Activity: The Robot Kitchen Demonstration**

**Step 1:** Show a manual process
- Task: "Write 150 blog posts"
- Time needed: 300 hours (2 hours Ã— 150)
- Cost: Very expensive

**Step 2:** Show the framework process
- Task: "Run /generate-daily-blogs"
- Time needed: 15 minutes AI + 2 hours review
- Cost: Much cheaper

**Interactive Poll:**
- "Would you rather spend 300 hours or 2 hours?"
- "What would you do with the extra 298 hours?"

**Part 3: The D/A/F Workflow (20 minutes)**

**Activity: Quality Control Simulation**

**Materials:** Print 10 sample "products" (could be simple drawings)
- 6 are good quality
- 4 are defective

**Task:**
1. Inspect each product
2. Sort into 3 bins:
   - D/ (Draft - not inspected yet)
   - A/ (Approved - good quality)
   - F/ (Failed - defective)

**Debrief:**
- How did you decide which bin?
- What criteria did you use?
- How is this like reviewing blog posts?

**Assessment:**
Quick quiz (5 questions, 2 minutes):
1. What does SEO stand for?
2. What percentage of work do you do in this framework? (Answer: 5%)
3. What does D/ mean? (Answer: Draft)
4. What does A/ mean? (Answer: Approved)
5. What does F/ mean? (Answer: Failed)

**Homework:**
Read Module 1 from EDUCATIONAL-CURRICULUM.md

### Lesson 1.2: Framework Components (1 hour)

**Learning Objective:**
Students will be able to identify and explain the 3 main parts: Commands, Agents, Skills.

**Materials Needed:**
- Access to framework files
- File explorer open
- Printed handout: "Framework Map"

**Lesson Plan:**

**Part 1: The Food Delivery Analogy (15 minutes)**

**Activity: Role Play**

**Assign roles:**
- Student 1: Customer (gives commands)
- Student 2: Delivery driver (agent)
- Student 3: Chef (skill/recipe)

**Scenario:**
1. Customer says: "I want pizza" (command)
2. Driver receives order (agent loads)
3. Driver goes to restaurant (agent executes skill)
4. Chef makes pizza following recipe (skill instructions)
5. Driver delivers pizza (output)

**Discussion:**
- Who makes the decisions? (Customer)
- Who does the work? (Driver + Chef)
- Who follows instructions? (Chef/skill)
- Where do you fit in? (You're the customer!)

**Part 2: Explore the Framework Structure (30 minutes)**

**Activity: Scavenger Hunt**

**Checklist (students complete):**

**Time limit:** 20 minutes

**Competition:** First student to complete wins a prize

**Debrief (10 minutes):**

- What surprised you?
- What questions do you have?

**Activity: Create a Flowchart**

**Task:** Draw the journey from command to output

  - You (user)

**Learning Objective:**
Students will be able to review draft content and make approve/reject decisions.

**Materials Needed:**
- Access to sample client folder
- 10 draft blog posts (prepared in advance)
- Decision worksheet (provided below)

**Decision Worksheet Template:**

```
â–¡ Very confident
â–¡ Somewhat confident
â–¡ Not confident (need help)
```

**Lesson Plan:**

**Part 1: Review Criteria Training (15 minutes)**

**Activity: Good vs Bad Examples**

**Show Example 1: GOOD POST**
```markdown
---

description: "Learn financing options..."
keywords: [relevant keywords]
---
```

**Ask students:**
- What's good about this?
- Would you approve?

**Show Example 2: BAD POST**
```markdown

---

call us now we are the best

[Only 200 words, no structure, poor grammar]
```

**Ask students:**
- What's wrong with this?
- Would you reject?

**Part 2: Practice Reviews (30 minutes)**

**Activity: Review 10 Drafts**

**Setup:**
- Prepare 10 draft files in advance
- Mix of good (6), borderline (2), bad (2)
- Students review independently

**Instructions:**
1. Open first draft
2. Fill out decision worksheet
3. Make your decision (A/ or F/)
4. Move to next draft
5. Time limit: 3 minutes per draft

**Part 3: Group Discussion (15 minutes)**

**Activity: Compare Decisions**

**For each draft:**
- Poll class: "Who approved? Who rejected?"
- Discuss differences
- Explain reasoning
- Reach consensus

**Learning Points:**
- What criteria matter most?
- How to make decisions quickly?
- When to ask for help?

**Assessment:**
**Speed Test:**
- Give 5 new drafts
- 2 minutes per draft (total 10 minutes)
- Must reach 80% accuracy (4/5 correct decisions)

**Scoring Rubric:**
- 5/5 correct = Expert level (A+)
- 4/5 correct = Proficient (A)
- 3/5 correct = Developing (B)
- 2/5 correct = Needs practice (C)

**Homework:**
Review 20 drafts from sample clients, submit decision log
