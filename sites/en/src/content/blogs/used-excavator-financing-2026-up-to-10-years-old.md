### When New Makes Sense

Consider new if:
• Long-term government contract (5+ years) requiring reliability guarantee
      • Manufacturer warranty critical (can't afford downtime)
      • Custom specifications needed (special attachments, modifications)
      • Tax rebate utilization (capital allowances maximized)

Consider used if:
• Starting business (capital preservation critical)

      • Short-term contracts (1-3 years) where new machine ROI is poor
      • Budget-conscious (want lowest total cost)
      • Experienced with equipment maintenance (can handle repairs)

## What to Look For: Used Excavator Inspection

### 1. Undercarriage Condition

Check These Components:
• Track pads: Wear limit? Replacement cost RM15,000-RM25,000
      • Rollers and idlers: Play? Noise? Leaks?
      • Sprockets: Wear pattern? Tooth condition?
      • Track tension: Proper adjustment?
      Rule of Thumb: Undercarriage should have 40%+ life remaining. If tracks need replacement soon, negotiate price reduction.

### 2. Engine Performance

Check These Items:
• Cold start: Difficult? Excessive smoke?
      • Idle: Smooth? Missing? Rough?
      • Full power: Acceleration responsive?
      • Oil pressure: Within spec?
      • Coolant: Clean? No oil mixing?
      • Exhaust: Excessive black smoke? (burning oil)
      Test Drive: Operate for 30 minutes. Monitor temperature, power, unusual sounds.

### 3. Hydraulic System

Check These Points:
• Cylinders: Leaks? Rod pitting?
      • Hoses: Cracks? Bulges? Leaks?
      • Pump noise: Whining? Cavitation?
      • Functions: All smooth? Slow? Jerky?
      Function Test: Cycle all functions (boom, stick, bucket, swing, travel). Any hesitation or weakness indicates hydraulic issues.

### 4. Cabin & Electronics

Check These Features:
• Dashboard: All gauges working?
      • A/C: Cooling properly? (RM3,000-RM5,000 to fix)
      • Joysticks: Smooth? Loose?
      • Seat: Condition? Adjustments working?
      • Safety systems: Emergency stop? Lockout?

### 5. Service History

Ask For Documentation:
• Service records: Regular maintenance?
      • Oil analysis: Any issues detected?
      • Major repairs: What was replaced?
      • Hour meter: Actual hours verified?
      Red Flags: Missing service history, inconsistent hour readings, signs of major repairs not documented.

## Used Excavator Age: What's Optimal?

### 1-3 Years Old: Sweet Spot

Advantages:
• Still under manufacturer warranty (sometimes transferable)
      • Modern technology (fuel-efficient, Tier-compliant)
      • Low hours (typically 3,000-6,000 hours)
      • Like-new condition at 70-80% of new price
      • Long remaining useful life (8,000+ hours)

Best For:
• Contractors wanting near-new at lower cost
      • Long-term projects (5+ years)
      • Businesses sensitive to downtime risk

### 4-6 Years Old: Best Value

Advantages:
• Significant depreciation already absorbed
      • Price: 50-65% of new
      • Proven track record (reliability known)
      • Most major depreciation over
      • 5,000-8,000 remaining useful hours

Best For:
• Budget-conscious contractors
      • Fleet expansion (multiple machines)
      • 3-5 year project horizons

### 7-10 Years Old: Lowest Price

Advantages:
• Lowest purchase price (35-50% of new)
      • Fastest equity building
      • Suitable for shorter-term use
      • Can be profitable even with higher maintenance

Best For:
• Startups with limited capital
      • Specific short-term contracts
      • Contractors capable of DIY maintenance

Risks to Manage:
• Higher maintenance costs (RM10,000-RM20,000/year)
      • Potential for major repairs (engine, hydraulics)
      • Shorter remaining useful life (3,000-5,000 hours)
      • May need replacement sooner
