## Mini Excavator Financing: Small Contractor Approval Guide

### What Financiers Look For

1. Contractor Registration & CIDB Grade:
• CIDB Grade: Preferably Grade B or higher (small contractors often start at Grade C)
      • Valid business registration (SSM)
      • Active track record (at least 6 months)
      Don't Have CIDB Yet? We can still finance if you have:
        - Letter of award for upcoming project
        - Signed contracts with clients
        - Proof of previous experience (even as subcontractor)

2. Project Pipeline:
• Signed contracts: Best approval chances
      • Letter of intent: Good (subject to confirmation)
      • Verbal agreements: May require additional documentation
      No Pipeline Yet? First-time contractor approval still possible with:
        - Strong personal financial position
        - Experience working for other contractors
        - Co-signer or guarantor (close family member)

3. Machine Utilization Plan:
• Expected monthly usage hours
      • Types of projects (residential, commercial, government)
      • Operating area (Klang Valley, Johor, Penang, etc.)
      Tip: Be specific. "3 residential foundation projects monthly in Klang Valley" is stronger than "general construction."

### Required Documents

For Registered Contractors:
• SSM business registration
      • CIDB certificate (if available)
      • Bank statements (6 months)
      • Project contracts or letters of award
      • Director's NRIC (front and back)

For New Contractors (Starting Out):
• SSM business registration
      • Director's NRIC (front and back)
      • Bank statements (personal or business, 6 months)
      • Letter of award or project quotation
      • Proof of previous experience (letters from past employers/clients)

## Mini Excavator Selection: Right Size for Your Work

### 1-1.8 Ton: Ultra-Compact

Best For:
• Backyard excavation (residential)
      • Narrow gateways (less than 1 meter wide)
      • Indoor demolition (can fit through doorways)
      • Trenching for utilities (water, electricity)

Typical Projects:
• Residential sewer line replacement (RM3,000-RM8,000 per job)
      • Small pond excavation (RM2,000-RM5,000 per job)
      • Garden grading and leveling (RM1,500-RM4,000 per job)
      • Concrete demolition in confined spaces (RM2,000-RM6,000 per job)

Limitations:
• Limited digging depth (typically 2-2.5 meters)
      • Lower bucket capacity (slower for large volume)
      • Not suitable for heavy-duty excavation

### 2-3 Ton: Sweet Spot for Small Contractors

Best For:
• Residential foundations (single and double story)
      • Drainage and pipe laying (medium depth)
      • Small commercial renovation
      • Landscaping projects

Typical Projects:
• Single-story house foundation (RM8,000-RM15,000 per job)
      • Drainage installation (100-200 meters, RM5,000-RM12,000 per job)
      • Swimming pool excavation (small, RM10,000-RM20,000 per job)
      • Commercial shop lot renovation (RM12,000-RM25,000 per job)

Why This Size Is Popular:
• Versatile (handles most residential work)
      • Affordable (RM85,000-RM110,000 new)
      • Easy to transport (can tow with 3-ton truck)
      • Low operating costs

### 4-5 Ton: Medium Duty Specialist

Best For:
• Double-story house foundations
      • Commercial building foundation (small)
      • Road trenching (utility installation)
      • Medium-scale earthmoving

Typical Projects:
• Double-story house foundation (RM15,000-RM30,000 per job)
      • Commercial foundation (2-3 stories, RM25,000-RM50,000 per job)
      • Road trenching (utilities, RM8,000-RM18,000 per day)
      • Parking lot excavation (RM20,000-RM40,000 per job)

Higher Revenue Potential:
• Can charge 30-50% more than 1-3 ton machines
      • Takes on larger, more profitable contracts
      • Faster completion (higher bucket capacity)

### 6-8 Ton: Bridge to Large Excavators

Best For:
• Commercial construction (medium scale)
      • Industrial foundation work
      • Road and drainage projects
      • Large residential developments

Typical Projects:
• Industrial factory foundation (RM50,000-RM150,000 per job)
      • Large drainage projects (municipal, RM30,000-RM80,000 per job)
      • Road construction (sub-base preparation, RM40,000-RM100,000 per job)
      • Condominium excavation (RM80,000-RM200,000 per job)

Contractor Upgrade Path:
• Start with 3-5 ton, build reputation
      • Upgrade to 6-8 ton as contracts grow
      • Add 20-ton+ excavator for large infrastructure

## Common Small Contractor Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Buying Too Large Too Fast

Problem: New contractor buys 6-8 ton excavator for residential work.
      Result: Machine is too big for most residential sites (can't access through gates). Underutilized.
      Better Approach: Start with 2-3 ton (versatile), upgrade as contracts grow.

### Mistake 2: Insufficient Working Capital

Problem: Uses all available cash for down payment. No money left for operations.
      Result: Can't afford fuel, operator, or materials. Machine sits idle.
      Better Approach: 0% down payment financing preserves cash for operations.

### Mistake 3: No Project Pipeline

Problem: Buys machine without confirmed projects. Hopes work will come.
      Result: Machine sits idle for months. Installment payments drain cash.
      Better Approach: Secure at least 2-3 months of work before financing machine.

### Mistake 4: Ignoring Used Equipment Options

Problem: Only considers new machines. Used machines are "too risky."
      Result: Higher financing costs, longer payback period.
      Better Approach: Used machines (3-6 years old) offer 40-60% savings with reliable performance if maintained well.
