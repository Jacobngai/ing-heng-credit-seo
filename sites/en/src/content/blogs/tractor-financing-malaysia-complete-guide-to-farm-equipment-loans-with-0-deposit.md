## Professional Inspection Process

Every used tractor requires comprehensive agricultural equipment inspection before approval:

### Detailed Agricultural Inspection Scope

**Engine &amp; Powertrain Assessment (40% weighting):**

• Compression test (all cylinders measured under load)

• Oil analysis (quality, contamination levels, metal particles)

• Coolant system (leaks, temperature regulation, radiator condition)

• Turbo functionality (if equipped - most modern tractors)

• Transmission performance (gear changes, clutch operation, hydraulic response)

• PTO operation (power take-off engagement and disengagement)

• Differential operation (front/rear axle, locking mechanisms)

• Exhaust emissions (agricultural compliance check)

**Hydraulic &amp; Implement Systems (25% weighting):**

• Three-point hitch operation (lift capacity, stability)

• Remote hydraulic outlets (flow rate, pressure)

• Loader operation (if equipped - lift capacity, curl function)

• Hydraulic fluid condition (contamination, level, pressure)

• Cylinder sealing (lift arms, tilt cylinders, steering)

• Control valve responsiveness (joystick operation, detents)

**Chassis &amp; Agricultural Components (20% weighting):**

• Frame integrity (stress cracks, weld quality)

• Front axle (steering, king pins, wheel bearings)

• Rear axle (differential, final drives, brake system)

• Tire condition (tread depth, even wear, sidewall integrity)

• Wheel weights and ballast (proper weight distribution)

• ROPS/FOPS certification (safety structure integrity)

**Electrical &amp; Safety Systems (15% weighting):**

• Battery condition (capacity, charging system)

• Lighting (work lights, hazard lights, turn signals)

• Instrument cluster (hour meter, gauges, warning lights)

• Air conditioning (operator comfort during long days)

• Safety equipment (seat belts, safety switches)
