# Injection Molding Machine Financing Malaysia: Scale Your Factory Without Draining Cash
Upgrading your factory's capacity is one of the fastest ways to grow your manufacturing business. But if you’ve looked at the price of a new plastic injection molding machine lately, you know that a high-quality molder — whether it’s from Haitian, Chen Hsong, or Arburg — comes with a significant price tag.
For most Malaysian SMEs, paying RM200,000 to RM800,000 upfront in cash just isn't practical. It drains your working capital, leaving you with no safety net for raw materials, payroll, or electricity bills.
That’s where **injection molding machine financing** comes in. By spreading the cost over several years, you can put the machine to work immediately, let it pay for itself through increased output, and keep your cash reserves intact.
## Why Finance Your Injection Molding Machine?
In manufacturing, "cash is king" but "output is growth." If you wait three years to save up enough cash for a new molder, you’re losing three years of potential revenue and market share to competitors who are willing to finance their expansion.
Here’s why smart factory owners choose financing:
1. **Preserve Working Capital:** Keep your cash for operations, marketing, and emergency repairs.
2. **Immediate ROI:** The machine starts producing parts (and profit) from Day 1, often covering its own monthly installment.
3. **Tax Benefits:** In Malaysia, you can claim **Capital Allowance** on your machinery, reducing your company's taxable income.
4. **Hedge Against Inflation:** Lock in today's equipment price and pay for it with tomorrow's Ringgit.
## Types of Financing for Injection Molding Machines
In Malaysia, there are three main ways to fund your next machine purchase:
### 1. Industrial Hire Purchase (HP)
This is the most popular option for SMEs. The machine itself acts as the security for the loan. You pay a monthly installment over 3 to 5 years, and once the final payment is made, you own the machine 100%. At Ing Heng Credit, we often provide up to 100% financing for qualified businesses, meaning you might not even need a down payment.
### 2. Finance Leasing
Leasing is similar to hire purchase, but technically the lender owns the machine and you "rent" it for the term. This can sometimes offer different tax treatments (check with your accountant). At the end of the lease, you usually have the option to buy the machine at a nominal price.
### 3. Term Loans
A traditional bank loan that might be secured by other assets (like your factory building or personal property). These often have lower interest rates but the approval process is much slower and the documentation requirements are much stricter.

## How to Get Approved Quickly
Getting a "Yes" from a lender doesn't have to be a headache. Here is what we look for when evaluating an injection molding machine loan:
* **Business Stability:** We typically look for businesses that have been operating for at least 1-2 years.

## Tax Benefits You Shouldn't Ignore
In Malaysia, purchasing industrial machinery like an injection molder allows you to claim **Capital Allowance (CA)**.
* **Initial Allowance (IA):** Usually 20% of the cost.
* **Annual Allowance (AA):** Usually 14% to 20% depending on the machine type.
In the first year alone, you might be able to deduct up to 40% of the machine's cost from your taxable profit. For a RM300,000 machine, that’s a RM120,000 deduction. This tax saving can significantly offset your loan interest costs.
