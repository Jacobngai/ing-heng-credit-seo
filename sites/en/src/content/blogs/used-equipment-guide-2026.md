## Used Equipment Inspection Checklist

### Visual Inspection

- Body condition: No major damage or rust
- Undercarriage: Check for wear and damage
- Tires/tracks: Good condition, proper tread
- Leaks: No oil, fluid, or hydraulic leaks

### Operational Test

- Start engine: Should start easily
- Test functions: All controls work properly
- Lift test: For excavators, forklifts
- Drive test: Moves smoothly, no unusual sounds

### Documentation Check

- Service records: Regular maintenance history
- Previous ownership: Clear title, no disputes
- Registration: Valid JPJ/APAD documents
- Inspection reports: Recent condition checks

## Used Equipment Risks and How to Avoid

### Risk: Hidden Problems

Solution:
• Professional inspection before buying
      • Test all functions thoroughly
      • Check service history
      • Get warranty if possible

### Risk: High Maintenance Costs

Solution:
• Budget for repairs and maintenance
      • Choose reliable brands
      • Avoid overly worn equipment
      • Set aside maintenance reserve

### Risk: Short Remaining Life

Solution:
• Check operating hours
      • Assess condition carefully
      • Consider equipment age vs. price
      • Plan for eventual replacement
