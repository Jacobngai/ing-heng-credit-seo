## The Hidden Lorry Costs: Insurance and Road Tax

Every logistics business owner knows the pain. You budget RM180,000 for a lorry, finance RM150,000, and think you're set. Then the bills arrive:

First-year insurance:RM12,000-RM18,000
      Road tax:RM3,000-RM5,000
      JPJ PUSPAKOM inspection:RM500-RM800
      APAD permit renewal:RM300-RM500

Total First-Year Additional Cost:RM15,800-RM24,300

This isn't a one-time cost—it recurs annually. And these costs increase annually as your fleet grows.

The question: Should you pay these costs upfront or bundle them into your lorry financing?

## Breaking Down Lorry Insurance Costs

### What Determines Insurance Premiums?

Lorry Type and Value:
• 1-ton lorry: RM3,000-RM5,000/year
      • 5-ton lorry: RM8,000-RM12,000/year
      • 10-ton lorry: RM12,000-RM18,000/year

Cargo Type:
• General goods: Standard rates
      • Hazardous materials: +30-50%
      • Cold chain (refrigerated): +20-30%
      • Valuable cargo (electronics, pharmaceuticals): +40-60%

Operational Factors:
• Driver age and experience (younger = expensive)
      • Claims history (more claims = higher premiums)
      • Fleet size (discounts available for 5+ vehicles)
      • Security systems (GPS, dash cams reduce premiums)

### Malaysian Insurance Market

Leading insurers:
      • Kurnia, Tokio Marine, Allianz, MSIG
      • General insurance providers (Hong Leong, Etiqa, Takaful operators)

Typical Coverage:
• Third-party liability (mandatory): Minimum RM10 million coverage
      • Own damage (comprehensive): Actual lorry value
      • Windshield cover (usually included with comprehensive)
      • Passenger liability (if carrying workers)

## Road Tax: The Annual Fixed Cost

### Road Tax Structure by Lorry Type

1-Ton Lorry: RM600-RM900/year
      3-Ton Lorry: RM1,200-RM1,800/year
      5-Ton Lorry: RM2,000-RM3,000/year
      10-Ton Lorry:RM3,500-RM5,000/year

### Calculation Method

Road tax is calculated based on:
      • Lorry weight (unladen weight)
      >• Engine capacity (cc rating)
      • Seating capacity (number of passengers)
      >• Lorry type (private vs commercial, flatbed vs box body)

Payment Schedule:
• Due annually by January 31
      • Late payment penalty: 2-4% of amount due
      • Non-payment results in JPJ warrant suspension (lorry can't operate legally)

## Additional Compliance Costs: What Else You Need to Pay

### JPJ PUSPAKOM Inspection (Annual/Bi-Annual)

Required For: All commercial vehicles
      Cost:RM500-RM800 per inspection
      Frequency: Every 6 months (most vehicles) or annually (some older vehicles)
      Purpose: Roadworthiness and safety inspection

### APAD Permit (License)

Required For: All commercial transport
      Cost:RM300-RM500 annually
      Purpose: Goods vehicle license
      Note: Permit must be displayed on lorry at all times

### Commercial Vehicle License (LKV)

Required For: Lorries with gross weight exceeding 3.5 tons
      Cost:RM50-RM100 annually
      Purpose: Additional licensing for heavy vehicles

## The Cash Flow Problem: Front-Loaded Annual Costs

### The Cash Crunch Reality

For a 5-lorry fleet:
      • Annual insurance: RM50,000 (RM10,000 × 5)
      • Annual road tax: RM15,000 (RM3,000 × 5)
      • JPJ PUSPAKOM: RM3,000 (RM600 × 5)
      • APAD permits: RM2,000 (RM400 × 5)
      Total Annual Recurring Cost:RM70,000

Monthly Impact: RM5,833 in fixed costs before fuel, drivers, or maintenance.

### Traditional Payment Problem

Most operators pay these costs in lump sums:
      • Insurance: January (lump sum RM50,000)
      • Road tax: January (lump sum RM15,000)

Result:First quarter (January-March) has RM65,000+ in due payments—before factoring in installments, fuel, and salaries.

## Insurance Optimization: How to Reduce Premiums

### Strategy 1: Fleet Discounts

Insurers offer 10-20% discounts for fleets of 5+ vehicles:
      • Standard single policy: RM10,000/year
      • Fleet discount (5 lorries): RM8,000-9,000/year
      Annual Savings:RM5,000-10,000 per policy

### Strategy 2: Safety Features Reduce Premiums

Invest in safety technology (often required by financing anyway):
      • GPS tracking: -5% to -10% premium
      • Dash cameras: -5% to -8% premium
      • Speed limiters: -3% to -5% premium
      • Driver training programs: -3% to -5% premium

Technology Cost vs Premium Savings:
• GPS unit: RM1,000 one-time, saves RM500-1,000 annually
      • Dash cam: RM800 one-time, saves RM400-800 annually
      ROI: 1-2 years to recover cost, then pure savings

### Strategy 3: Higher Deductibles = Lower Premiums

Accept higher deductibles to reduce premiums:
      • Standard deductible: RM1,000 per claim
      • High deductible: RM5,000-RM10,000 per claim
      • Premium reduction: 15-25%

Caveat: Ensure you can afford RM10,000 out-of-pocket per claim before choosing high deductibles.

## Financing vs Paying Cash: Which Makes Sense?

### Pay Cash Approach

Pros:
• No interest costs
      • Full ownership of insurance policies
      • Can change insurers annually

Cons:
• Large upfront cash outflow (RM70,000+ annually)
      • Capital tied up in prepayments
      • Inflation increases these fixed costs over time
      • Opportunity cost (cash could earn returns elsewhere)

### Financing (Bundled) Approach

Pros:
• No large upfront cash outflow
      • Fixed monthly payment simplifies budgeting
      • Interest tax-deductible (business expense)
      • Preserves working capital for operations

Cons:
• Interest costs apply
      • Less flexibility to change insurers annually

### Decision Framework

If Paying Cash:
• Requires consistent cash reserves
      • Must have RM70,000+ available annually for insurance/tax
      • Capital could earn returns if invested elsewhere

If Financing (Bundled):
• 0% down payment preserves capital
      • Single payment simplifies cash flow management
      >• Interest cost often offset by investment returns on preserved capital
