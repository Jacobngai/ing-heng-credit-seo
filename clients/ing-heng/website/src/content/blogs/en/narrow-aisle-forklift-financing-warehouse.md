## What Are Narrow Aisle (VNA) Forklifts?



      

Standard forklifts require aisles 3.5-4.5 meters wide to maneuver. Narrow aisle (also called VNA - Very Narrow Aisle) forklifts operate in aisles as narrow as 1.5-2.1 meters.



      

### How VNA Forklifts Achieve Narrow Operation



      

- **Rear-Wheel Steering:** Turns like a shopping cart, pivoting around the front axle
- **Mast Rotation:** Forks rotate 90-180 degrees, allowing sideways load handling
- **Compact Design:** Smaller turning radius, reduced tail swing
- **Camera Systems:** Operators see what's behind without turning
- **Wire Guidance:** Some VNA models follow floor-mounted wires for precise aisle alignment



      

### Types of Narrow Aisle Forklifts



      

**VNA Reach Trucks:**
      
• Aisle width: 1.6-2.1 meters
      
• Lift height: Up to 13 meters
      
• Capacity: 1-2.5 tons
      
• Best for: Pallet storage, high-bay racking systems



      

**VNA Turret Trucks:**
      
• Aisle width: 1.5-1.8 meters
      
• Lift height: Up to 15 meters
      
• Capacity: 1-1.5 tons
      
• Best for: Maximum density, automated systems



      

**Articulated VNA Forklifts:**
      
• Aisle width: 1.8-2.3 meters
      
• Can operate in both narrow aisles and standard warehouse areas
      
• Best for: Mixed operations (some narrow aisles, some standard)



      

## The Space Efficiency Math: What VNA Really Saves



      

### Example: 10,000 Sq Ft Warehouse



      

**Standard Forklift Layout (3.5m aisles):**
      
• Warehouse: 50m × 30m = 1,500 sq m
      
• Aisles: 8 aisles × 3.5m × 30m = 840 sq m
      
• Storage racks: 660 sq m
      
**Storage Efficiency:** 44% (660 ÷ 1,500)



      

**VNA Forklift Layout (2m aisles):**
      
• Warehouse: 50m × 30m = 1,500 sq m
      
• Aisles: 12 aisles × 2m × 30m = 720 sq m
      
• Storage racks: 780 sq m
      
**Storage Efficiency:** 52% (780 ÷ 1,500)



      

**Result:** 18% more storage in same footprint (780 vs 660 sq m)

## When VNA Makes Sense: Applications and Considerations



      

### Ideal for VNA Implementation



      

- **High-Rent Urban Areas:** Klang Valley, Penang, Johor Bahru where warehouse space is expensive
- **Cold Storage:** Refrigerated space costs 3-4x standard rates—maximizing density is critical
- **E-Commerce Fulfillment:** High SKU count, fast-moving inventory benefits from dense storage
- **Long-Term Leases:** If committed to 5+ years in same facility, VNA ROI compounds
- **Vertical Expansion:** VNA forklifts reach higher (12-15 meters) than standard (6-8 meters)



      

### Less Ideal for VNA



      

- **Low-Ceiling Warehouses:** VNA benefits decrease below 6-meter racking height
- **High Mix of Floor-Loaded Items:** If 50%+ of handling is at floor level, narrow aisles won't help
- **Frequent Layout Changes:** VNA requires fixed racking—reconfiguring is expensive
- **Very Low Storage Density Needs:** If warehouse is half-empty, maximizing density is unnecessary
