Understanding Malaysia's Driver Shortage
          

          


            The haulage industry is facing a critical challenge: **approximately 5,000 drivers short** across cargo lorries, express buses, and transport vehicles. This shortage impacts every aspect of container haulage operations.
          



          
            Root Causes of the Shortage
          

          
            

- 
                  


                
                **Aging workforce** - Most drivers nearing retirement, few young replacements
- 
                  
                
                **Migration to Singapore** - Better salaries attracting experienced drivers
- 
                  
                
                **High entry costs** - Goods Driving Licence costs RM4,000-5,000
- 
                  
                
                **High turnover costs** - Replacing drivers costs tens of thousands RM
- 
                  
                
                **Operational challenges** - Long hours, stressful conditions, limited rest


          

          
            Impact on Haulage Rates
          

          
            The driver shortage directly affects your bottom line and pricing strategy:
          



          

- **Higher wage demands** - Drivers know they're in demand and negotiate better pay
- **Operational disruptions** - Delayed deliveries when drivers are unavailable
- **Training costs** - New drivers require time and investment to become productive
- **Competition for talent** - Poaching between hauliers drives up costs



          


            Many hauliers respond by **increasing haulage rates** to cover these costs. However, this can make you less competitive. The solution is **operational efficiency** through better equipment and technology.
          



          
            Driver Retention Strategies
          

          
            
              
                1
              
              
                

#### Invest in Comfortable Trucks


                

Newer prime movers (Scania, Volvo, Mercedes) offer better cabs, air-conditioning, and ergonomics. Drivers prefer comfortable vehicles and stay longer with fleets that invest in their working conditions.


              
            
            
              
                2
              
              
                

#### Implement GPS Fleet Tracking


                

GPS systems reduce driver stress by optimizing routes, providing proof of delivery, and eliminating paper documentation. Drivers appreciate technology that makes their jobs easier.


              
            
            
              
                3
              
              
                

#### Fair Scheduling & Rest Periods


                

Avoid scheduling drivers for 28 days a month. Provide adequate rest, predictable schedules, and respect for work-life balance. Burnout drives good drivers away.


              
            
            
              
                4
              
              
                

#### Career Progression Path


                

Create clear advancement opportunities. Senior drivers can become trainers, fleet managers, or partners. Show drivers a future with your company beyond just driving.


              
            
            
              
                5
              
              
                

#### Assist with License Costs


                

Consider helping drivers with Goods Driving Licence fees (RM4,000-5,000) in exchange for work commitments. This builds loyalty and reduces turnover.

            Alternative Perspective: Efficiency vs. Shortage
          

          


            Some industry experts argue the problem isn't a true shortage but **inefficiency**. Empty lorries running on any given day reduce available driver capacity. Better load optimization and route planning could alleviate pressure without hiring more drivers.
          



          


            GPS tracking systems help identify inefficiencies by:
          



          

- Tracking empty return trips and optimizing backhaul opportunities
- Identifying inefficient routes and suggesting alternatives
- Monitoring driver behavior to reduce fuel waste and vehicle wear
- Providing data for better scheduling and dispatch decisions
