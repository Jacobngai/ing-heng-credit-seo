## Why Traditional Banks Demand Collateral (And Why We Don’t)

Banks operate on a simple principle: minimize risk by taking maximum security. They don’t want to understand your business model, analyze your contracts, or assess your industry experience. They want tangible assets they can seize if things go wrong.

**What Banks Typically Demand as Collateral:**

• Property titles (residential or commercial)

• Fixed deposits (often 110% of loan amount)

• Personal guarantees from directors

• Life insurance policies assigned to bank

• Inventory or equipment as floating charge

• Multiple guarantors with property

**The Hidden Trap of Collateral Banking:**
When you pledge your property for business financing, you’re not just securing a loan - you’re potentially losing everything if your business faces temporary challenges. One bad quarter, one delayed payment from a major client, one industry downturn could cost you your home.
