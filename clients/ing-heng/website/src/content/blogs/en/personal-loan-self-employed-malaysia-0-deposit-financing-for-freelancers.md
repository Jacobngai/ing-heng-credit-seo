## Why Traditional Banks Discriminate Against Self-Employed Professionals

### The Fixed Salary Bias

Banks are built around employee mindset. They want to see:

• Same employer for 2+ years

• Fixed monthly salary

• EPF contributions

• Leave benefits and medical coverage

**They don’t understand self-employed success:**

• Multiple income streams provide MORE security

• Direct client relationships mean business control

• Profit margins often exceed employee salaries

• Financial discipline required for business survival

### The Documentation Trap

Banks demand standardized paperwork that doesn’t fit freelancer reality:

**What banks want:**

• EA form from employer

• 3-month salary slips

• EPF statement

• Employment letter

**What self-employed people have:**

• Business bank statements

• Client invoices and contracts

• Income tax returns (Form B/BE)

• Professional service agreements

### The Income Averaging Problem

Banks look at your worst month and panic. They don’t understand:

• **Seasonal variations** (wedding photographers earn more in certain months)

• **Project-based income** (consultants complete large projects quarterly)

• **Retainer plus project model** (agencies have base income plus variable projects)

• **Growth trajectories** (income increasing year-over-year despite monthly fluctuations)
