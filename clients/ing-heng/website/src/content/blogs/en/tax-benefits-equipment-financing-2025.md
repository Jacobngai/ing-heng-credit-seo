## The Tax Advantage: Financing Creates Deductions



      

Every profitable Malaysian business wants to reduce their tax burden legally. Equipment financing offers powerful tax benefits that purchasing outright doesn't provide.



      

The key difference: when you pay cash for equipment, you get one deduction (the equipment itself). When you finance equipment, you get multiple deductions (equipment depreciation + interest + fees) spread over years.



      

This doesn't mean financing is "better" than buying—it means financing provides more opportunities to reduce taxable income over time. For growing businesses, this can significantly reduce cash outflows to LHDN.



      

## The Tax Mechanism: What You Can Deduct



      

### Hire Purchase (Financing) Deductions



      

**1. Capital Allowance (Depreciation):**
      
• Year 1: 40% of equipment cost (Initial Allowance)
      
• Year 2 onwards: 20% of equipment cost (Annual Allowance)
      
• Claimed against business income each year



      

**Example: RM200,000 Excavator Financed**
      
• Year 1 deduction: RM80,000 (40%)
      
• Year 2 deduction: RM40,000 (20%)
      
• Year 3 deduction: RM40,000 (20%)
      
• Year 4 deduction: RM40,000 (20%)
      
• Year 5 deduction: RM0 (fully depreciated)
      
**Total Deductions:** RM200,000 (100% of cost)



      

**2. Interest Payments:**
      
• 100% of financing interest is tax-deductible business expense
      
• Each monthly installment includes interest portion
      
• Annual interest deducted from business income



      

**Example (6% Interest, 5-Year Term):**
      
• Total interest paid: ~RM60,000
      
• Annual deduction: ~RM12,000
      
• All deductible against business income



      

**3. Related Expenses:**
      
• Insurance premiums: 100% deductible
      
• Road tax: 100% deductible
      
• Maintenance and repairs: 100% deductible
      
• Loan fees (processing, documentation): deductible



      

### Cash Purchase Deductions



      

**Capital Allowance Only:**
      
• Year 1: 40% deduction
      
• Year 2+: 20% annual deduction
      
• Total: RM200,000 deduction over 5 years (same as financing)



      

**What You Miss:**
      
• No interest deduction (you paid no interest)
      
• Upfront deduction vs. spread over time (financing provides consistent annual deductions)



      

## The Tax Timing Advantage: Smoothing Deductions



      

### Scenario: High Profit Year



      

**Business Situation:**
      
• Year 1 profit: RM500,000 (high due to large contract)
      
• Year 2 profit: RM300,000 (normal operations)
      
• Need equipment: RM200,000 excavator



      

**Option A: Cash Purchase in Year 1**
      
• Year 1 deduction: RM80,000 (40% of RM200k)
      
• Taxable income: RM420,000
      
• Tax at 24%: ~RM100,800
      
• Years 2-5: Additional RM40,000 deductions annually



      

**Option B: Finance (5-Year Term)**
      
• Year 1 deduction: RM80,000 (depreciation) + RM12,000 (interest) = RM92,000
      
• Taxable income: RM408,000
      
• Tax at 24%: ~RM97,900
      
• Years 2-5: RM40,000 (depreciation) + RM12,000 (interest) = RM52,000 annually



      

**Result:** Financing saves RM2,900 in Year 1 taxes AND provides consistent RM52,000 deductions in Years 2-5 when income may be lower. Cash purchase provides RM40,000 annually.



      

## Capital Allowance Rates (2025)



      

### Current Rates Under Income Tax Act 1967



      

**Initial Allowance (Year 1):**
      
• 20% of asset cost for most equipment
      
• 40% for "heavy machinery" (excavators, bulldozers, cranes, prime movers, lorries)
      
• Claim in year of acquisition



      

**Annual Allowance (Years 2+):**
      
• 20% of asset cost annually
      
• Claimed each year until fully depreciated



      

### Equipment Categories & Rates



      

**40% Initial Allowance (Heavy Machinery):**
      
• Excavators, bulldozers, loaders, graders
      
• Cranes, lorries, trucks, prime movers
      
• Industrial forklifts (3 tons+)
      
• Manufacturing equipment (CNC machines, production lines)



      

**20% Initial Allowance (Light Equipment):**
      
• Small forklifts (under 3 tons)
      
• Power tools, hand tools
      
• Office equipment, computers



      

### Special Allowances (Accelerated Depreciation)



      

**Pickup Trucks & Vans (Under 3,500kg):**
      
• 40% initial allowance
      
• Special higher rates for certain vehicles (check latest LHDN guidelines)



      

**Automation Equipment:**
      
• Industry 4.0 automation may qualify for special incentives
      
• Check MIDA (Malaysian Investment Development Authority) for specific incentives

## Hire Purchase vs. Operating Lease: Tax Comparison



      

### Hire Purchase (Ownership)



      

**Deductions:**
      
✓ Capital allowance (depreciation)
      
✓ Interest payments
      
✓ Insurance, maintenance, repairs



      

**Ownership:**
      
✓ Asset on balance sheet
      
✓ Builds business net worth
      
✓ Residual value after depreciation



      

### Operating Lease (Rental)



      

**Deductions:**
      
✓ Monthly rental payments (100% deductible)
      
✓ No depreciation (you don't own asset)
      
✓ No interest deduction (no loan)



      

**Limitations:**
      
✗ No ownership (asset builds less business value)
      
✗ Usage restrictions (often limited hours/year)
      
✗ No residual value (return asset at end)



      

**Tax Advantage Comparison:**
      
• Hire Purchase: Higher total deductions (capital allowance + interest)
      
• Lease: Consistent deductions but lower total
      
**Winner:** Hire purchase offers better tax deductions for profitable businesses



      

## 0% Down Payment: Preserving Tax Deduction Capacity



      

### Traditional Financing (20% Down Payment)



      

**RM300,000 Prime Mover:**
      
• Down payment: RM60,000
      
• Financed: RM240,000
      
• Capital allowance on: RM240,000 (not RM300,000)



      

**Tax Impact:**
      
• Year 1 deduction: RM96,000 (40% of RM240k)
      
• RM24,000 deduction lost (down payment portion)



      

### 0% Down Payment Financing



      

**Same RM300,000 Prime Mover:**
      
• Down payment: RM0
      
• Financed: RM300,000
      
• Capital allowance on: RM300,000 (full amount)



      

**Tax Impact:**
      
• Year 1 deduction: RM120,000 (40% of RM300k)
      
• Maximum deduction preserved



      

**Additional Benefit:**
      
• RM60,000 preserved capital earns interest or generates revenue
      
• Can be invested in business operations (generates more deductible expenses)

## Common Tax Mistakes to Avoid



      

### Mistake 1: Not Claiming All Deductions



      

Many businesses claim only equipment cost deduction but forget:
      
• Interest portion of installments (separate principal and interest)
      
• Insurance and road tax (fully deductible)
      
• Maintenance and repairs (even minor repairs add up)



      

### Mistake 2: Mixing Business and Personal Use



      

Equipment used personally (e.g., director takes lorry home weekends):
      
• Portion of deduction disallowed (personal use ratio)
      
• Complex calculation required
      
• LHDN may audit and disallow portion of deductions



      

### Mistake 3: Not Keeping Proper Records



      

LHDN requires evidence for every deduction claimed:
      
• Missing invoices = deduction disallowed
      
• Unsegregated expenses (personal + business) = complications
      
• Incomplete asset register = audit risks



      

### Mistake 4: Wrong Depreciation Rates



      

Using incorrect allowance rates:
      
• Applying 20% to equipment qualifying for 40%
      
• Not claiming special allowances for eligible equipment
      
• Missing accelerated depreciation incentives
