## Common Concerns and How to Address Them

### ”Will I Be Approved?”

Most first-time borrowers with stable employment and adequate income are approved by lenders specializing in this market. The key is choosing the right lender and presenting a complete, accurate application.

**Success Factors:**

• Stable employment for at least 3-6 months

• Income sufficient to support comfortable loan repayment

• Complete documentation and accurate application

• Choosing lenders that welcome first-time borrowers

### ”What Interest Rate Will I Pay?”

First-time borrowers often access competitive rates, especially with lenders competing for new customers. Rates are typically based on overall application strength rather than penalizing lack of credit history.

**Rate Optimization:**

• Shop around with multiple lenders

• Highlight employment stability and income growth potential

• Consider longer-term relationship benefits

• Ask about promotional rates or new customer incentives

### ”Do I Need a Guarantor?”

Many modern lenders don’t require guarantors for first-time borrowers with adequate income and employment stability. 0% deposit options are often available without additional guarantees.

**Guarantor Alternatives:**

• Stable employment documentation

• Higher income-to-loan ratios

• Shorter loan terms

• Relationship banking with existing accounts

### ”How Much Can I Borrow?”

Borrowing capacity is typically 3-8 times monthly income, but varies based on employment stability, income level, and lender criteria. Many first-time borrowers are surprised by how much they can access.

**Maximizing Borrowing Capacity:**

• Document all income sources thoroughly

• Highlight career progression and stability

• Demonstrate financial responsibility

• Choose lenders with flexible criteria
