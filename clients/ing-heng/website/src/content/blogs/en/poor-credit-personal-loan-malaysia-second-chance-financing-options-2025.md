## Understanding Poor Credit in Malaysia

Poor credit refers to a low credit score or negative payment history that makes traditional banks hesitant to approve loan applications.

### What Constitutes Poor Credit:

**CTOS Credit Scores:**

• Score 300-499: Very poor credit

• Score 500-649: Poor to fair credit

• Multiple late payments or defaults

• High debt utilization (over 80%)

**CCRIS Ratings:**

• Rating 3: Special mention (potential problems)

• Rating 4: Substandard (significant payment issues)

• Rating 5: Doubtful (serious collection problems)

**Common Poor Credit Situations:**

• Multiple credit card late payments

• Personal loan defaults

• Car loan payment issues

• Utility bill payment delays

• PTPTN loan payment problems

• Business loan payment difficulties

## Why Traditional Banks Reject Poor Credit Applications

Banks operate under strict lending guidelines that prioritize low-risk borrowers. Understanding their perspective helps explain why alternative lenders exist.

### Bank Rejection Reasons:

**Credit Score Thresholds:**

• Most banks require CTOS scores above 650

• CCRIS ratings must be 1 or 2

• No recent defaults or late payments

• Clean credit history for 12-24 months

**Risk Management Policies:**

• Banks answer to regulators and shareholders

• Bad debt provisions affect profitability

• Computer systems auto-reject poor credit applications

• Limited flexibility in underwriting criteria

**Documentation Requirements:**

• Extensive income verification

• Employment confirmation letters

• Multiple guarantors often required

• Collateral or security needed

### The Reality Gap:

Many Malaysians with temporary financial setbacks or past mistakes still have stable incomes and genuine repayment ability. Banks’ rigid criteria create a gap that specialized lenders fill.

## Red Flags: Avoiding Predatory Lenders

Poor credit borrowers are often targeted by unscrupulous lenders. Learn to identify and avoid predatory practices.

### Warning Signs:

**Upfront Fee Demands:**

• Requesting payment before approval

• “Processing fees” or “guarantee deposits”

• Insurance purchases required upfront

• Administrative charges before loan disbursement

**Unrealistic Promises:**

• “100% approval regardless of credit”

• “No credit check required”

• “Instant approval and disbursement”

**Pressure Tactics:**

• Limited-time offers requiring immediate decision

**Check Licensing:**

• Verify company registration with SSM

## Frequently Asked Questions About Poor Credit Loans

**Q: Will applying for a poor credit loan hurt my credit score further?**

A: Each loan application creates a credit inquiry, which can temporarily lower your score by 5-10 points. However, multiple inquiries for the same type of loan within a 14-45 day period are typically counted as a single inquiry. The impact is minimal compared to the benefit of obtaining financing and making on-time payments.

**Q: Should I pay off old debts before applying for a new loan?**

A: If you can afford to settle outstanding debts before applying, it will strengthen your application significantly. However, if you need the loan to consolidate and pay off debts, explain this clearly in your application. Many lenders approve consolidation loans for this specific purpose.

**Q: Can I get a personal loan if I’m currently in a debt management program?**

A: It’s challenging but not impossible. Some lenders will consider applications from borrowers in AKPK or other debt management programs, especially if you’ve been making payments consistently and are near completion of the program. Be transparent about your situation.

**Q: How soon can I apply for a larger loan amount after successfully repaying a poor credit loan?**

A: After 12-24 months of successful payments, you can typically apply for larger amounts with better terms. Your improved payment history with the lender, plus credit score improvements, will strengthen your application significantly.

**Q: Are there government programs to help with poor credit situations?**

A: While there are no direct government loan programs for poor credit, AKPK (Credit Counselling and Debt Management Agency) provides free financial counseling and debt management plans. Successfully completing their program can help improve your creditworthiness for future applications.
