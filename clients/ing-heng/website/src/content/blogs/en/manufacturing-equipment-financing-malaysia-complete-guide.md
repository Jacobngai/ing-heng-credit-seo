## How to Get Manufacturing Equipment Financing Approved

### Step 1: Know Your Equipment Needs

Before approaching any lender, clarify:
- Exact equipment specifications needed
- New vs used preference
- Budget range
- Expected production output/ROI

**Example:** "Need CNC VMC with 500mm x 400mm table, 10,000 RPM spindle, for aerospace component work generating RM50,000/month in new contracts."

### Step 2: Gather Documentation

**Required:**
- SSM registration (Form 9/24/49)
- 6-12 months bank statements
- Equipment quotation with full specifications
- Business profile or company introduction

**Helpful additions:**
- Current customer contracts
- Production capacity analysis
- Maintenance records (for used equipment)

### Step 3: Demonstrate Business Viability

Lenders want to know you can repay. Show:
- Current revenue and cash flow
- Contracts that will use the new equipment
- Growth projections based on added capacity
- Industry demand in your sector

**Strong application:** "This RM200,000 CNC machine will fulfill RM800,000 in annual contracts from 3 existing customers who currently source from competitors due to our capacity constraints."

### Step 4: Choose the Right Financing Structure

**Hire Purchase:**
- You own the equipment after final payment
- Equipment serves as primary collateral
- Fixed monthly payments
- Best for: Equipment you'll use long-term

**Lease-to-Own:**
- Rental payments with purchase option
- May have lower monthly payments initially
- Flexibility to upgrade
- Best for: Technology that changes quickly

**Term Loan:**
- Lump sum disbursement
- Can be used for multiple equipment pieces
- May require additional collateral
- Best for: Large equipment packages

## Avoiding Common Manufacturing Financing Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Underestimating Total Equipment Cost

**Problem:** Budget RM200,000 for CNC machine, forget installation, training, tooling, software.

**Reality:** Additional costs often add 15-25% to equipment price.

**Solution:** Include all costs in financing request—installation, commissioning, initial tooling, operator training.

### Mistake 2: Choosing Equipment Based Only on Price

**Problem:** Buy cheapest machine available, face reliability issues, downtime, poor part quality.

**Solution:** Balance cost against reliability, service availability, and production quality. A RM200,000 Japanese machine with 20-year lifespan beats a RM100,000 no-brand machine that fails in 5 years.

### Mistake 3: Ignoring Maintenance Requirements

**Problem:** Finance equipment without budgeting for ongoing maintenance, spare parts, service contracts.

**Solution:** Factor maintenance costs into cash flow projections. Ask equipment dealers about typical annual maintenance expenses.

### Mistake 4: Not Matching Equipment to Actual Needs

**Problem:** Buy 500-ton injection molding machine when 150-ton would suffice. Overpay on equipment AND financing.

**Solution:** Analyze actual production requirements. Consult equipment specialists. Right-size your purchase.

### Mistake 5: Waiting for "Perfect" Conditions

**Problem:** Wait until you have 30% down payment saved. Takes 18 months. Competitors capture market share.

**Solution:** 0% down payment options exist. Finance now, compete now.
