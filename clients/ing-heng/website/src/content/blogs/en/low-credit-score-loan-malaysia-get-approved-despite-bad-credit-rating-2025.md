## Understanding Credit Scores in Malaysia

Credit scores in Malaysia are calculated by two main credit bureaus: CTOS and CCRIS (Central Credit Reference Information System). Understanding how these systems work helps you better position your loan application.

### CTOS Credit Score Breakdown:

**Score Ranges and Ratings:**

• 851-1000: Excellent credit

• 751-850: Very good credit

• 651-750: Good credit

• 500-650: Fair credit (border line)

• 300-499: Poor credit (low score)

• Below 300: Very poor credit

**What Affects Your CTOS Score:**

• Payment history (35% of score)

• Credit utilization ratio (30% of score)

• Length of credit history (15% of score)

• Types of credit accounts (10% of score)

• New credit inquiries (10% of score)

### CCRIS Rating System:

**Rating Classifications:**

• Rating 1: Excellent payment performance

• Rating 2: Satisfactory payment performance

• Rating 3: Special mention (potential problems)

• Rating 4: Substandard (significant issues)

• Rating 5: Doubtful (serious collection problems)

### Common Causes of Low Credit Scores:

**Payment Related Issues:**

• Late credit card payments

• Missed personal loan installments

• Defaulted car loans or mortgages

• Unpaid utility bills

• PTPTN loan payment delays

• Outstanding telecommunications bills

**High Debt Utilization:**

• Using over 80% of credit card limits

• Multiple active credit facilities

• High debt-to-income ratio

• Revolving credit dependencies

**Credit History Problems:**

• Short credit history (less than 2 years)

• Frequent credit applications

• Multiple credit inquiries in short periods

• Lack of diverse credit types

## Why Banks Reject Low Credit Score Applications

Traditional banks have strict lending criteria designed to minimize default risk. Understanding their perspective helps explain why alternative lenders are often your best option.

### Bank Rejection Criteria:

**Automated Scoring Systems:**

• Computer systems auto-reject low scores

• No human evaluation for borderline cases

• Rigid minimum score requirements (usually 650+)

• No consideration for circumstances

**Risk Management Policies:**

• Banks answer to regulators like Bank Negara

• Bad debt provisions affect bank profitability

• Shareholders expect low default rates

• Conservative lending mandates from management

**Documentation Requirements:**

• Extensive income verification needed

• Employment confirmation letters required

• Multiple guarantors often demanded

• Collateral or security preferred

### The Assessment Gap:

Many Malaysians with low credit scores still have:

• Stable employment and income

• Genuine reasons for past credit issues

• Current ability to make loan payments

• Improved financial circumstances

This creates an opportunity for specialized lenders who look beyond just credit scores.

## Building Credit Score While Repaying

Use your loan approval as an opportunity to rebuild your credit standing for future financial needs.

### Payment Excellence:

**Always Pay On Time:**

• Set up automatic payments to avoid late fees

• Pay slightly above minimum amount when possible

• Make payments early when you have extra cash

• Keep detailed records of all payments made

**Payment Strategy Tips:**

• Use payroll deduction if employer offers it

• Set multiple payment reminders

• Pay bi-weekly instead of monthly (reduces interest)

• Round up payments to nearest RM50

### Credit Management:

**Monitor Your Progress:**

• Check CTOS score every 6 months

• Review CCRIS updates quarterly

• Track improvements in credit utilization

• Document positive payment history

**Avoid Credit Damage:**

• Don’t apply for new credit unnecessarily

• Keep old credit accounts open (for history)

• Maintain low balances on existing cards

• Don’t close accounts immediately after paying off

### Financial Discipline:

**Budget Management:**

• Create and stick to monthly budget

• Build emergency fund (3-6 months expenses)

• Reduce unnecessary spending

• Increase income through side work if possible

**Debt Reduction Plan:**

• Focus on high-interest debts first

• Consider debt consolidation opportunities

• Avoid taking on additional debt

• Plan for early loan settlement when possible

## Red Flags: Avoiding Predatory Lending

Low credit score borrowers are often targeted by unscrupulous lenders. Learn to identify and avoid dangerous situations.

### Warning Signs of Predatory Lenders:

**Upfront Fee Scams:**

• Demanding payment before loan approval

• “Guarantee fees” or “processing deposits”

• Insurance purchases required before disbursement

• Administrative charges paid upfront

**Unrealistic Promises:**

• “Guaranteed approval for any credit score”

• “No credit check required”

• “Instant approval and same-day money”

• “Bad credit no problem, 100% approved”

**High-Pressure Tactics:**

• Limited-time offers requiring immediate decisions

• Refusing to provide written terms and conditions

• Discouraging you from shopping around

• Pushy sales tactics or threats

**Unclear or Hidden Terms:**

• Vague explanations of interest rates and fees

• Confusing contract language

• Hidden charges not disclosed upfront

• No proper loan agreement documentation

### Verification Steps:

**Company Legitimacy:**

• Verify business registration with SSM

• Check money lender license (if applicable)

• Confirm physical office address

• Read online reviews and complaints

**Term Transparency:**

• Request all terms in writing before deciding

• Calculate total cost of loan including all fees

• Understand penalty clauses and charges

• Verify interest rate calculation method

**Professional Standards:**

• Professional office environment and staff

• Proper loan documentation and processes

• Clear communication and explanations

• Reasonable time for decision-making

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How much can I borrow with a CTOS score of 400?**

A: With a CTOS score of 400, you can typically borrow RM3,000 to RM20,000 depending on your monthly income, employment stability, and debt service ratio. Some lenders may approve higher amounts if you provide guarantors or collateral. The key is demonstrating current repayment ability rather than focusing solely on the credit score.

**Q: Will my credit score improve after successfully repaying a low credit score loan?**

A: Yes, successful repayment of any loan improves your credit score over time. Each on-time payment contributes positively to your payment history, which is the largest factor in credit score calculations. You may see gradual improvements after 6-12 months of consistent payments.

**Q: Should I wait to improve my credit score before applying for a loan?**

A: It depends on your urgency. If you can wait 6-12 months and work on improving your score naturally, you may qualify for better rates. However, if you need financing urgently, apply now with specialized lenders who accept low credit scores, then use the loan repayment to rebuild your credit.

**Q: Can I refinance my low credit score loan later for better terms?**

A: Yes, after 12-24 months of successful payments and credit score improvement, you may qualify to refinance with better terms. Many borrowers start with higher-rate loans and refinance to lower rates once their creditworthiness improves.

**Q: What’s the minimum income required for low credit score loans?**

A: Most lenders require minimum monthly income of RM1,500-RM2,000 for personal loans. However, the more important factor is your debt service ratio - your total monthly debt payments should not exceed 60% of your monthly income, including the new loan payment.
