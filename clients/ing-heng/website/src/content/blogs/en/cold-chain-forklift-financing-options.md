## Why Standard Forklifts Fail in Cold Storage



      

### 1. Battery Performance Collapse



      

Standard forklift batteries lose 40-60% capacity in sub-zero environments:
      
• At -18°C: Battery operates at 50-60% of rated capacity
      
• At -25°C: Battery operates at 40-50% of capacity
      
• Result: 2-hour runtime becomes 1 hour or less



      

### 2. Hydraulic System Failure



      

Standard hydraulic fluid thickens in cold:
      
• Viscosity increases 5-10x at -20°C
      
• Pump strain increases dramatically
      
• Component wear accelerates 3-5x
      
• Result: Premature hydraulic pump failure



      

### 3. Steel Brittleness



      

Standard steel becomes brittle in extreme cold:
      
• Fork arms can crack under load (catastrophic failure risk)
      
• Mast components may fracture
      
• Structural integrity compromised over time



      

### 4. Electronics Failure



      

Standard electronics aren't cold-rated:
      
• Displays freeze or become unreadable
      
• Control systems malfunction
      
• Sensors fail or give inaccurate readings



      

### 5. Operator Safety Hazard



      

Standard forklifts in cold storage create unsafe conditions:
      
• Unpredictable performance (reduced braking, slower response)
      
• Sudden failure risks (dropping frozen loads is hazardous and expensive)
      
• Operator discomfort (standard cabs aren't insulated)
