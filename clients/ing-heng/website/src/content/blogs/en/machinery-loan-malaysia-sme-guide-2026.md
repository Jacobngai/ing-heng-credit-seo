## What Banks Won't Tell You About Machinery Loans

Banks follow rigid policies. Here's what disqualifies most SME applications:

### Equipment Age Restrictions

Banks typically cap equipment age at 5 years. That 8-year-old Komatsu excavator running perfectly? Automatic rejection.

**Reality:** Well-maintained machinery lasts 15-20+ years. Age alone doesn't determine value or reliability.

### Down Payment Requirements

Bank machinery loans require 20-30% down. For a RM300,000 bulldozer, that's RM60,000-90,000 you need upfront.

**Problem:** Most SMEs can't lock up that much cash without affecting operations.

### Credit Score Obsession

Banks want CTOS 650+. One late utility payment three years ago? Score drops. Application rejected.

**Alternative approach:** Some lenders evaluate your business contracts, cash flow, and equipment condition instead of just CTOS numbers.

## How to Improve Your Machinery Loan Approval Chances

### 1. Prepare Strong Documentation

Before applying anywhere, gather:
- SSM registration (Form 9/Form 24/Form 49)
- 6-12 months bank statements
- Current business contracts or purchase orders
- Equipment quotation with specifications
- Company profile (optional but helpful)

**Pro tip:** Showing RM500,000 in upcoming contracts matters more to alternative lenders than your credit score.

### 2. Know Your Equipment Value

Research fair market prices before applying. If you're quoted RM180,000 for a used excavator, verify that's reasonable for the model, year, and condition.

Lenders protect themselves—if equipment is overpriced, they'll reduce financing or reject the application.

### 3. Demonstrate Revenue Potential

Answer these questions in your application:
- How much monthly revenue will this equipment generate?
- What contracts or projects will use it?
- Is there consistent demand for this machinery type?

**Example:** "This forklift will serve our 3 warehouse contracts generating RM35,000/month. Monthly payment would be RM2,500—well within cash flow."

### 4. Consider Equipment Condition Over Age

A 10-year-old Caterpillar with full service records, new tracks, and rebuilt engine is better collateral than a 3-year-old no-brand machine with unknown maintenance history.

Alternative lenders understand this. Banks often don't.

## Common Mistakes That Kill Machinery Loan Applications

### Mistake 1: Applying Only to Banks

Banks reject 40-60% of equipment financing applications. Don't stop there.

**Solution:** Apply to banks AND alternative lenders simultaneously. Compare offers.

### Mistake 2: Underestimating Documentation Needs

"I'll submit bank statements later" leads to delays and rejections.

**Solution:** Have ALL documents ready before your first inquiry.

### Mistake 3: Overreaching on Loan Amount

Requesting RM500,000 when your business generates RM50,000/month raises red flags.

**Solution:** Keep monthly payments below 15-20% of monthly revenue.

### Mistake 4: Ignoring Equipment Inspection

Lenders will inspect or appraise equipment. Hidden problems cause loan rejections or reduced amounts.

**Solution:** Inspect equipment yourself first. Get service records. Know what you're buying.

### Mistake 5: Not Explaining Your Business Case

Lenders aren't mind readers. A bare application with just numbers doesn't tell them why you'll succeed.

**Solution:** Include a brief explanation of how you'll use the equipment and generate income.
