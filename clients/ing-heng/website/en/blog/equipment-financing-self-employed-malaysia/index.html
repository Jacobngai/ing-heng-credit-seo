The Self-Employed Contractor Challenge

Why Banks Struggle with Self-Employed Applications

Traditional Banking Mindset:

  • Payslips = reliable income
  • Company benefits = reduced risk
  • Formal structure = trustworthy business

  • Cash transactions common in construction

The Disconnect: Banks designed their assessment for salaried employees, not skilled independent contractors.

Common Self-Employed Financing Barriers

Income Documentation:

  • No formal payslips to show regular salary
  • Cash payments difficult to document completely
  • Seasonal fluctuations appear risky to banks
  • Project completion cycles create income gaps

Business History:

  • Recently registered businesses lack track record
  • Previous employment experience not recognized as business capability
  • Client relationships considered personal, not business assets
  • Industry expertise not quantified in financial terms

Financial Patterns:

  • Irregular cash flow patterns
  • Higher proportion of cash transactions
  • Project-based expense patterns
  • Variable monthly income and expense cycles
