The Working Capital Preservation Strategy

Understanding Working Capital vs Equipment Investment

Working capital definition and importance:

Working capital is the cash available for day-to-day business operations - the difference between current assets and current liabilities. It funds inventory purchases, covers operating expenses during slow periods, and provides flexibility to capitalize on opportunities or weather unexpected challenges.

Cash vs financing impact comparison:

Cash Purchase Impact:

  • Upfront cost: RM90,000 (full equipment cost)
  • Working capital reduction: RM90,000 immediately unavailable
  • Monthly cash flow: No ongoing payments, but limited operational flexibility
  • Opportunity cost: Cannot use RM90,000 for revenue-generating activities

Financing Impact:

  • Upfront cost: RM0 (with zero deposit financing)
  • Working capital preservation: Full RM90,000 remains available
  • Monthly commitment: RM1,800-2,500 depending on terms
  • Opportunity benefit: RM90,000 available for growth investments
