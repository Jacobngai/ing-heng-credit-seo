Complete Guide to Lorry Loans in Malaysia 2025: Fast Approval & Competitive Rates

Running a logistics or transport business in Malaysia means owning your own lorry is key to success. But buying a lorry requires a large amount of capital. This is why lorry loans have become the preferred solution for many logistics business owners.

Whether you are a new entrepreneur or an established owner looking to expand your fleet, understanding the detailed lorry loan process can help you make the best decisions.

This article will explain all the details about lorry loans in Malaysia, including application processes, required documents, eligibility requirements, and practical advice.

Why Do You Need a Lorry Loan?

Build Your Own Fleet

Renting a lorry costs up to RM2,000-3,500 per month. Over the long term, this expense can buy several lorries.

By financing a lorry purchase, you can:

  • Own an asset: The lorry belongs to you and can be resold or refinanced
  • Reduce costs: Monthly payments are usually cheaper than rental
  • Expand business: Accept more contracts and earn more income
  • Build credit: On-time payments can build a good credit record

Seize Business Opportunities

When you receive large orders or transport contracts, you need to increase your lorries immediately. If you wait for bank approval (usually takes 2-4 weeks), you might lose the contract.

Fast approval lorry loans can get you funding within 1-3 business days, allowing you to seize opportunities.

Preserve Cash Flow

If you pay for the lorry in full, your company‚Äôs cash flow will be affected. If customers delay payment, you might face cash shortages.

Through financing, you can:

  • Preserve cash for daily expenses (fuel, repairs, salaries)
  • Handle emergencies (vehicle breakdowns, urgent repairs)
  • Invest in other business opportunities

Types of Lorry Loans in Malaysia

1. Hire Purchase Plan

This is the most common lorry financing method.

How it works:

  • You pay a down payment (usually 10-30%)
  • The remaining amount is paid in installments (36-84 months)
  • After paying all amounts, the lorry is yours

Advantages:

  • Lower application threshold
  • Can finance used lorries (up to 10 years old)
  • Fixed monthly payments, easy to budget

Suitable for:

  • Sole proprietors
  • Small logistics companies
  • Owners wanting to buy used lorries

2. Business Loan

If your company is registered (Sdn Bhd), you can apply for a business loan to buy lorries.

Advantages:

  • Higher loan amounts
  • Can purchase multiple lorries at once
  • More tax benefits

Suitable for:

  • Registered companies
  • Logistics companies wanting bulk purchases
  • Enterprises needing large financing

3. Leasing Plan

Similar to renting a vehicle, but with an option to purchase at the end of the lease.

How it works:

  • Pay monthly rent
  • Choose to purchase or return at the end of the lease
  • Suitable for short-term needs

Suitable for:

  • Seasonal businesses
  • Owners wanting to try different vehicle models
  • Enterprises not wanting to hold long-term assets

Step 1: Assess Your Needs

Before applying for a loan, ask yourself a few questions:

What type of lorry do I need?

  • 1-ton small lorry (delivery, courier)
  • 3-5 ton medium lorry (building materials, groceries)
  • 10-ton large lorry (containers, bulk cargo)

  • New vehicle price: RM60,000-350,000

  • Affordable monthly payment: Recommended not to exceed 30% of income

How much can I pay as down payment?

  • Minimum 10-20% (most lenders require)
  • Higher down payment means lower monthly payments and less interest

Common Lorry Loan Questions

1. Can I apply without a formal company?

Yes! We accept applications from sole proprietors.

You only need:

  • Business license (SSM)
  • Business bank statements (showing income)
  • Identity card and residential proof

2. My CTOS score is very low, can I still get approval?

Yes! Our approval rate reaches high approval rate.

Even if your credit score is below 600, we will comprehensively assess:

  • Your actual repayment ability
  • Business income stability
  • Down payment amount
  • Guarantor situation

Suggestions:

  • Provide detailed income proof
  • Increase down payment amount
  • Find guarantor with good credit

3. How much can I borrow?

This depends on several factors:

Sole Proprietors:

  • Loan amount is usually 3-5 times monthly income
  • Example: Monthly income RM5,000, can borrow RM15,000-25,000

Companies (Sdn Bhd):

  • More flexible loan amount
  • Assessed based on company turnover and financial status
  • Can apply for loans above RM100,000

4. How long after loan approval can I collect the vehicle?

New vehicle:

  • 3-7 business days after signing contract
  • Wait for dealer delivery and JPJ registration

Used vehicle:

  • 1-3 business days after signing contract
  • Can collect after transfer procedures completed

Emergency situations: If you have urgent transport contracts, we can expedite the process. Please inform us when applying.

5. What happens if I delay payment?

Late 1-15 days:

  • Usually charge late payment fee (1-2%)
  • Will not immediately affect credit record
  • We will contact you to understand the situation

Late 16-30 days:

  • Late payment fee increases
  • May be reported to CTOS/CCRIS
  • Need to contact us to negotiate repayment plan

Late more than 30 days:

  • Seriously affects credit record
  • May face legal action
  • Lorry may be repossessed

Suggestion: If you foresee repayment difficulties, contact us in advance. We can:

  • Rearrange repayment plan
  • Extend loan term (reduce monthly payment)
  • Temporarily reduce interest (special circumstances)

6. Can I pay off the loan early?

Yes! But some financing companies charge prepayment penalty.

Ing Heng‚Äôs Policy:

  • Prepayment in first 12 months: May charge small processing fee
  • Prepayment after 12 months: Usually no charge

Benefits:

  • Save remaining interest
  • Own lorry earlier
  • Can refinance or sell

7. Can I sell the lorry during the loan period?

Technically no, because before the loan is paid off, the lorry ownership belongs to the lender.

But you can:

  1. Pay off loan first then sell
  2. Transfer loan to buyer (need lender approval)
  3. Sell lorry and use proceeds to pay off loan

If you want to sell the lorry, please contact us first to discuss the best solution.

8. What insurance do I need to buy?

Must purchase:

  • Comprehensive Insurance: Covers accidents, theft, natural disasters
  • Third Party Insurance: Covers injury and property damage to others

Optional purchase:

  • Cargo insurance: Protects transported goods
  • Personal accident insurance: Protects driver and passengers

Insurance costs:

  • New vehicle: 2-4% of vehicle price/year
  • Used vehicle: 3-5% of vehicle price/year
  • Example: RM80,000 lorry, insurance about RM2,400-3,200/year
