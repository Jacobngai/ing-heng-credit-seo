Professional Mobile Crane Inspection Process

Comprehensive Assessment Standards

Every mobile crane financing application requires professional inspection:

Engine & Drivetrain Evaluation (35% weighting):

  • Compression testing: All cylinders measured for consistency
  • Oil analysis: Quality, contamination, and change intervals
  • Cooling system: Temperature regulation and leak assessment
  • Hydraulic pumps: Pressure testing and flow rates
  • Transmission: Gear engagement and shift quality
  • Final drives: Noise levels and lubricant condition
  • Exhaust analysis: Emissions compliance and engine health

Hydraulic System Assessment (30% weighting):

  • Main pumps: Flow rate and pressure testing
  • Boom cylinders: Extension/retraction speed and smoothness
  • Swing motor: Rotation speed and positioning accuracy
  • Outrigger systems: Extension force and stability
  • Hydraulic filtration: Filter condition and system cleanliness
  • Hose inspection: Age, flexibility, and leak assessment
  • Cylinder seals: Internal leakage and rebuild requirements

Structural & Mechanical Inspection (25% weighting):

  • Chassis integrity: Frame cracks, welds, and structural soundness
  • Boom sections: Extension/retraction mechanisms and wear
  • Counterweight: Secure mounting and balance verification
  • Outrigger boxes: Structural integrity and operation
  • Carrier tires: Condition, wear patterns, remaining life
  • Crane tires: Load capacity, age, and replacement needs
  • Load block assembly: Hook, block, and wire rope condition

Safety & Documentation Review (10% weighting):

  • Load charts: Current and accurate capacity information
  • Safety systems: Load moment indicators and alarms
  • Operator cab: Visibility, controls, and safety equipment
  • Certification: Current inspection certificates and compliance
  • Service history: Maintenance records and major repairs
  • Parts availability: Support network and replacement costs
