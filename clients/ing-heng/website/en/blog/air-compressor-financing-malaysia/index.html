  • Test automatic drain valves

Choosing the Right Air Compressor for Your Operations

  • Typical usage factor: 60-75% for most workshops
  • Effective demand: 50 CFM √ó 70% = 35 CFM actual requirement
  • Recommended compressor: 40-45 CFM capacity with storage tank

Step 3: Plan for growth and peak demand

  • Consider peak demand periods when multiple tools operate simultaneously
  • Account for compressed air quality requirements (moisture, oil content)

Compressor Type Selection Guide

Reciprocating compressors best for:

  • Intermittent duty cycles: Less than 60% runtime
  • Lower CFM requirements: Under 50 CFM typically
  • Budget-conscious applications: Lower initial cost
  • Simple maintenance: Easier to service locally

Rotary screw compressors best for:

  • Continuous operation: 80%+ runtime applications
  • Higher CFM requirements: 50-2000+ CFM

Oil-lubricated vs oil-free:

Q: How do I determine the right size compressor for my operation?

A: Proper sizing requires analyzing your specific compressed air demand:

Step 1: Equipment audit

  • List every pneumatic tool and its CFM requirement
  • Note the pressure requirement for each application
  • Determine usage patterns (simultaneous vs sequential operation)

Step 2: Usage factor calculation

  • Apply realistic usage factors (60-75% typical for most operations)
  • Account for peak demand periods when multiple tools operate
  • Consider future expansion and additional equipment

Step 3: Professional assessment

  • Our technical specialists can perform compressed air audits
  • We use compressed air demand calculators for accurate sizing
  • Consider duty cycle requirements (intermittent vs continuous)
  • Factor in air quality requirements (moisture, oil content)

Common sizing mistakes to avoid:

  • Undersizing: Forces compressor to work constantly, shortens life, poor tool performance
  • Oversizing: Higher initial cost, energy waste during light-load periods
  • Ignoring storage: Adequate receiver tank size smooths demand variations
