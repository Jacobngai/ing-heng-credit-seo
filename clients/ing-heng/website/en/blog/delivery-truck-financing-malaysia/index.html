Professional Delivery Vehicle Inspection

Every delivery truck requires comprehensive inspection before approval:

Delivery Vehicle Inspection Focus Areas

Delivery Capability Assessment (40% inspection weighting):

  • Load capacity verification (payload ratings, suspension adequacy)
  • Cargo area condition (floor, walls, securing points)
  • Loading equipment (tailgate, ramps, accessibility)
  • Cargo securing systems (tie-down points, safety equipment)
  • Access and maneuverability (turning radius, parking capability)

Operational Reliability Evaluation (35% inspection weighting):

  • Engine performance (power delivery, fuel efficiency)
  • Transmission operation (smooth shifting, appropriate gearing)
  • Braking system (stopping power, safety for loaded operation)
  • Electrical system (battery capacity, alternator output)
  • Cooling system (adequate for delivery stop-start operations)

Commercial Vehicle Standards (25% inspection weighting):

  • Commercial vehicle compliance (safety standards, regulations)
  • Driver visibility and safety (mirrors, lighting, visibility)
  • Professional appearance (suitable for customer-facing delivery)
  • Maintenance accessibility (service points, component access)
  • Documentation completeness (registration, permits, compliance)

Delivery Vehicle Inspection Grading

Grade A (Excellent Delivery Vehicle - Best Terms)

  • All delivery systems operating optimally
  • Professional maintenance history documented
  • Excellent cargo handling capability
  • Financing Impact: Best delivery vehicle rates, 0% deposit possible
  • Delivery Capability: Suitable for premium customer delivery services

Grade B (Good Delivery Vehicle - Standard Terms)

  • Minor maintenance items due but operational
  • Normal wear appropriate for delivery vehicle age
  • Adequate cargo capacity and handling systems
  • Financing Impact: Standard delivery vehicle rates apply
  • Delivery Capability: Suitable for standard delivery operations

Grade C (Acceptable Delivery Vehicle - Conditional)

  • Some repairs needed before optimal operation
  • Delivery capability present but requires attention
  • Cargo handling systems functional but need improvement
  • Financing Impact: Higher deposit or rate adjustment required
  • Delivery Capability: Limited to basic delivery applications

Grade D (Poor Delivery Vehicle - Usually Rejected)

  • Major systems failure affecting delivery capability
  • Safety systems compromised for delivery operations
  • Cargo handling systems inadequate or damaged
  • Financing Impact: Application typically declined
  • Recommendation: Major repairs or alternative vehicle selection

Common Delivery Vehicle Issues

Minor Issues (Usually Acceptable):

  • Tailgate hydraulic adjustment needed (RM 500-1,000)
  • Cargo area cleaning and minor repairs (RM 300-800)
  • Loading equipment lubrication and service (RM 200-500)
  • Minor body damage not affecting functionality (RM 1,000-2,000)

Moderate Issues (Require Attention):

  • Suspension component replacement for load capacity (RM 2,000-4,000)
  • Brake system service for commercial loading (RM 1,500-3,000)
  • Electrical system upgrade for commercial use (RM 1,000-2,500)
  • Cargo securing system repair or upgrade (RM 800-2,000)

Major Issues (Usually Disqualifying):

  • Engine inadequate for delivery operations (major rebuild required)
  • Transmission failure affecting cargo capacity
  • Structural damage affecting load-bearing capability
  • Safety systems compromised beyond commercial standards
