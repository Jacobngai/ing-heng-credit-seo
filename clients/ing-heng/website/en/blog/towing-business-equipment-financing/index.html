Frequently Asked Questions

What is the minimum credit score needed for towing equipment financing?

Most lenders require a CTOS score of 650 or higher for towing equipment financing. However, some specialized equipment financing companies may consider applications with scores as low as 600, especially if you have relevant industry experience or can provide additional collateral.

Can I finance a complete towing business setup including multiple vehicles?

Yes, many lenders offer fleet financing packages that can cover multiple tow trucks, support vehicles, and equipment in a single loan facility. This approach often provides better terms and simplified administration compared to individual vehicle loans.

How long does the equipment financing approval process typically take?

Standard bank loans usually take 2-4 weeks for approval and disbursement. Equipment financing companies typically process applications faster, with approvals possible in 3-7 working days for well-documented applications with strong credit profiles.

What insurance coverage is required for financed towing equipment?

Comprehensive motor insurance is mandatory, including coverage for the vehicle, equipment, and third-party liability. Many lenders also require business interruption insurance and may specify minimum coverage amounts. The financing company is typically listed as the first beneficiary.

Can I claim tax deductions for towing equipment financing costs?

Interest payments on equipment financing are generally tax-deductible as business expenses. Additionally, you may be eligible for capital allowances on the equipment purchase. Consult with a qualified accountant to understand the specific tax benefits available to your business structure.

Is it better to lease or purchase towing equipment?

This depends on your business model and financial situation. Purchasing (through financing) builds equity and provides full ownership benefits, while leasing offers lower monthly payments and easier equipment upgrades. Consider factors like usage intensity, technological advancement rates, and long-term business plans.

What happens if my towing business fails and I cannot repay the equipment loan?

The equipment typically serves as collateral for the loan, meaning the lender can repossess and sell it to recover outstanding amounts. However, you may remain liable for any shortfall between the sale proceeds and loan balance. It‚Äôs crucial to have adequate business planning and insurance coverage to mitigate these risks.

Can I add additional equipment to my existing financing agreement?

Many lenders offer top-up facilities that allow you to add new equipment to existing financing arrangements. This can provide better terms than separate loans and simplified administration. However, this depends on your payment history and current financial position with the lender.
