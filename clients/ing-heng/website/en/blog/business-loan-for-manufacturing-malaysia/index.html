Equipment Financing Options for Manufacturers

Hire Purchase Financing

How It Works: Manufacturing company selects required production equipment, lender purchases equipment and leases to company, company makes monthly payments over agreed term, ownership transfers after final payment.

Benefits for Manufacturers:

  • Immediate access to production equipment
  • Preserve working capital for operations
  • Fixed monthly payments for budget planning
  • Tax benefits through capital allowances
  • Build equipment equity through payments

Typical Terms:

  • Down payment: 10-30% (0% for qualified applicants)
  • Tenure: 12-84 months depending on equipment type
  • Interest rates: 7.5-12% per annum (reducing balance)
  • Equipment age: New or up to 10+ years old

Equipment Leasing

Operating Lease Benefits:

  • Lower monthly payments than hire purchase
  • Equipment upgrades and technology refresh
  • Maintenance and service often included
  • Off-balance-sheet financing structure
  • Flexible end-of-term options

Finance Lease Advantages:

  • Build equity while using equipment
  • Purchase option at favorable residual value
  • Tax benefits through depreciation
  • Equipment customization allowed
  • Long-term cost advantages

Asset-Based Lending

Using Manufacturing Assets:

  • Existing equipment as collateral base
  • Inventory financing secured by stock
  • Accounts receivable financing
  • Combined asset approach for larger facilities
  • Flexible credit lines for working capital

Benefits:

  • Higher borrowing limits based on asset values
  • Flexible repayment terms
  • Multiple use of funds (equipment, working capital, expansion)
  • Established manufacturers with substantial assets
  • Growth financing for expanding operations

Common Manufacturing Financing Challenges

Challenge 1: Technology Obsolescence Concerns

The Problem: Lenders worry about equipment becoming outdated or losing value quickly.

The Solution:

  • Choose established, proven technology with long useful life
  • Document equipment manufacturer support and upgrade paths
  • Show strong resale market for equipment type
  • Emphasize operational benefits beyond technology features
  • Provide conservative depreciation and residual value estimates

Challenge 2: Market Demand Variability

The Problem: Manufacturing businesses can be sensitive to economic cycles and customer demand changes.

The Solution:

  • Document diversified customer base and markets
  • Show historical performance through economic cycles
  • Highlight essential products with stable demand
  • Present market analysis supporting growth projections
  • Demonstrate flexibility and adaptability in operations

Challenge 3: Working Capital Complexity

The Problem: Manufacturing companies have complex working capital requirements that can concern lenders.

The Solution:

  • Focus on equipment financing rather than working capital loans
  • Show strong working capital management and controls
  • Demonstrate predictable cash flow patterns
  • Document supplier payment terms and customer collections
  • Present conservative cash flow projections including equipment payments
