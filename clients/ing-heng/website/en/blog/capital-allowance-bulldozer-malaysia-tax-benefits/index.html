What is Capital Allowance in Malaysia?

Capital allowance is the Malaysian tax system‚Äôs equivalent of depreciation, allowing businesses to claim tax deductions on capital expenditure over the asset‚Äôs useful life. Unlike accounting depreciation, capital allowance rates and methods are specifically defined by the Inland Revenue Board of Malaysia (LHDN).

Legal Framework:

Income Tax Act 1967:

  • Schedule 3: Machinery and equipment classification
  • Section 61: Initial Allowance (IA) provisions
  • Section 62: Annual Allowance (AA) calculations
  • Section 63: Accelerated capital allowance

LHDN Guidelines:

  • Public Ruling No. 1/2018: Capital allowance guidelines
  • Schedule 3 classification of construction equipment
  • Disposal and balancing allowance/charge rules

Types of Capital Allowance:

1. Initial Allowance (IA):

  • One-time deduction in first year of use
  • 20% of equipment cost for Schedule 3 items
  • Claimed in addition to Annual Allowance
  • Cannot be carried forward if insufficient income

2. Annual Allowance (AA):

  • Yearly depreciation deduction
  • 20% straight-line method for Schedule 3
  • Claimed every year until fully written off
  • Can be carried forward to future years

3. Accelerated Capital Allowance:

  • Enhanced incentives for specific industries/locations
  • Increased IA and AA rates for qualifying investments
  • Available for high-tech equipment in certain sectors

  • Full write-off period: 5 years

Qualifying Equipment:

  • Track-type bulldozers (D6, D7, D8, D9 series)
  • Wheel bulldozers
  • Crawler dozers with GPS technology

Financing vs Cash Purchase: Tax Comparison

Cash Purchase Tax Benefits:

Advantages:

  • Full capital allowance on entire purchase amount
  • No interest expense complications
  • Complete ownership from day one
  • Simpler tax calculations

Disadvantages:

  • Depletes working capital immediately
  • No additional interest deductions
  • Opportunity cost of cash deployment
  • Higher cash flow impact

Financing Tax Benefits:

Additional Tax Deductions:

  • Capital allowance on equipment cost
  • Plus loan interest expense deductions
  • Preserves working capital for operations
  • Leveraged tax benefits

Common Questions and Scenarios

Scenario 1: Mid-Year Equipment Purchase

Question: ‚ÄúI bought a bulldozer in July. Can I claim full-year capital allowance?‚Äù

Answer: Yes! Capital allowance is not pro-rated in Malaysia. You claim full IA and AA in the year the equipment is first used for business, regardless of purchase month. However, the equipment must actually be in business use to qualify.

Scenario 2: Equipment Upgrade/Modification

Question: ‚ÄúI spent RM80,000 upgrading my bulldozer with GPS technology. Is this capital allowance eligible?‚Äù

Answer: Yes, substantial improvements and upgrades qualify for capital allowance as separate assets or additions to the original cost basis. The upgrade must enhance the equipment‚Äôs capacity or efficiency for business use.

Scenario 3: Partnership/Joint Venture Equipment

Question: ‚ÄúOur joint venture bought equipment. How do we claim capital allowance?‚Äù

Answer: The partner who legally owns the equipment claims capital allowance. If jointly owned, claim in proportion to ownership percentage. Ensure clear ownership documentation and coordinate with all partners‚Äô tax advisors.

Scenario 4: Equipment Disposal

Question: ‚ÄúI sold my bulldozer after 3 years. What are the tax implications?‚Äù

Answer: You‚Äôll have a balancing allowance (if sold below written-down value) or balancing charge (if sold above written-down value). This adjusts your total capital allowance claim to reflect actual depreciation experienced.
