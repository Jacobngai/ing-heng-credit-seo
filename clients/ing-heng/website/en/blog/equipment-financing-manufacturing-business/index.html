What is Manufacturing Equipment Financing?

Manufacturing equipment financing is a specialized loan or hire purchase arrangement that allows businesses to acquire production equipment by paying over time instead of full cash upfront.

How it works:

  1. Choose your manufacturing equipment (new or used, up to 10 years old)
  2. Apply with 0-30% deposit depending on business profile
  3. Get approved within 1-3 business days
  4. Pay monthly installments over 12-84 months
  5. Own equipment after final payment (hire purchase)

Benefits for manufacturing businesses:

  • Preserve 70-100% of equipment cost for operations and raw materials
  • Start production immediately without waiting to accumulate cash
  • Tax deductible payments (capital allowances + interest deductions)
  • Fixed monthly payments for better cash flow planning
  • Equipment acts as collateral (no additional security needed)

Tips for Successful Manufacturing Equipment Financing

Before Applying

  1. Develop clear business case

    • Quantify productivity improvements expected
    • Calculate payback period and ROI
    • Identify specific customer demands driving need
    • Document competitive advantages gained

  2. Research equipment thoroughly

    • Compare multiple brands and models
    • Get quotes from 2-3 authorized dealers
    • Check user reviews and industry forums
    • Verify local service and support availability

  3. Organize financial documents

    • Recent bank statements showing strong cash flow
    • Financial statements demonstrating profitability
    • Customer contracts supporting revenue projections
    • Current equipment valuations and depreciation schedules

During Application Process

  1. Be responsive and professional

    • Answer lender questions promptly
    • Provide additional information when requested
    • Maintain clear communication throughout process
    • Show enthusiasm and knowledge about your business

  2. Demonstrate equipment necessity

    • Link equipment to specific customer contracts
    • Show capacity constraints with current equipment
    • Explain competitive pressures driving upgrade needs
    • Highlight safety or compliance requirements

  3. Present realistic financial projections

    • Conservative revenue growth assumptions
    • Include all costs: financing, maintenance, utilities
    • Show multiple scenarios (optimistic, realistic, pessimistic)
    • Demonstrate ability to service debt under all scenarios

After Approval

  1. Plan installation carefully

    • Schedule during slower production periods
    • Arrange employee training before installation
    • Prepare facility modifications needed
    • Have backup plans for production continuity

  2. Monitor performance closely

    • Track productivity metrics daily initially
    • Document efficiency improvements achieved
    • Measure quality improvements and waste reduction
    • Calculate actual ROI vs projections

  3. Build relationship with lender

    • Make payments on time consistently
    • Update lender on business growth and successes
    • Consider them for future equipment needs
    • Provide referrals when appropriate
