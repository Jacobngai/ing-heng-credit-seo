Professional Inspection Process

Every used tractor requires comprehensive agricultural equipment inspection before approval:

Detailed Agricultural Inspection Scope

Engine & Powertrain Assessment (40% weighting):

  • Compression test (all cylinders measured under load)
  • Oil analysis (quality, contamination levels, metal particles)
  • Coolant system (leaks, temperature regulation, radiator condition)
  • Turbo functionality (if equipped - most modern tractors)
  • Transmission performance (gear changes, clutch operation, hydraulic response)
  • PTO operation (power take-off engagement and disengagement)
  • Differential operation (front/rear axle, locking mechanisms)
  • Exhaust emissions (agricultural compliance check)

Hydraulic & Implement Systems (25% weighting):

  • Three-point hitch operation (lift capacity, stability)
  • Remote hydraulic outlets (flow rate, pressure)
  • Loader operation (if equipped - lift capacity, curl function)
  • Hydraulic fluid condition (contamination, level, pressure)
  • Cylinder sealing (lift arms, tilt cylinders, steering)
  • Control valve responsiveness (joystick operation, detents)

Chassis & Agricultural Components (20% weighting):

  • Frame integrity (stress cracks, weld quality)
  • Front axle (steering, king pins, wheel bearings)
  • Rear axle (differential, final drives, brake system)
  • Tire condition (tread depth, even wear, sidewall integrity)
  • Wheel weights and ballast (proper weight distribution)
  • ROPS/FOPS certification (safety structure integrity)

Electrical & Safety Systems (15% weighting):

  • Battery condition (capacity, charging system)
  • Lighting (work lights, hazard lights, turn signals)
  • Instrument cluster (hour meter, gauges, warning lights)
  • Air conditioning (operator comfort during long days)
  • Safety equipment (seat belts, safety switches)

Inspection Grading System for Agricultural Equipment

Grade A (Excellent - Best Terms)

  • All systems operating at full agricultural capacity
  • Recent major service completed with documentation
  • Complete maintenance history from agricultural operation
  • No structural or safety concerns for farm work
  • Financing Impact: Best rates available, 0% deposit possible
  • Approval: Virtually guaranteed for established farmers

Grade B (Good - Standard Terms)

  • Minor maintenance items due (normal for age/hours)
  • Normal wear appropriate for agricultural use
  • All safety systems functional for farm operations
  • Financing Impact: Standard agricultural rates apply
  • Approval: High probability for farming applications

Grade C (Acceptable - Conditional)

  • Some repairs needed before agricultural operation
  • Maintenance backlog evident but manageable
  • Functional but requires attention before heavy use
  • Financing Impact: Higher deposit or adjusted rates
  • Approval: Conditional on addressing agricultural safety issues

Grade D (Poor - Usually Rejected)

  • Major repairs required for safe agricultural operation
  • Safety systems compromised for farm work
  • Structural integrity concerns for farming loads
  • Financing Impact: Application typically declined
  • Recommendation: Seek alternative tractor or major repairs first

Common Agricultural Equipment Inspection Findings

Minor Issues (Usually Acceptable):

  • Hydraulic seal weepage (normal for age - RM 800-1,500 fix)
  • PTO clutch adjustment needed (RM 400-600 service)
  • Three-point hitch float adjustment (RM 200-400 calibration)
