Frequently Asked Questions

What size crane truck should I choose for general construction work in Malaysia?

For general construction, a 15-25 ton crane truck provides excellent versatility handling most building projects while maintaining road mobility. This capacity covers 70-80% of construction lifting needs while being economical to operate and finance. Consider your target market when selecting capacity.

How much can I expect to earn from crane truck operations?

Well-positioned crane truck operations achieve 60-75% utilization at daily rates of RM 800-2,200 depending on capacity and market. A 20-ton crane truck at 70% utilization and RM 1,200 average daily rate generates approximately RM 307,000 annually before operating costs.

What operator qualifications are required for crane truck operations?

Operators need appropriate commercial driving licenses, crane operation certificates, and safety training completion. Training costs range from RM 5,000-12,000 per operator. Most insurance companies require certification proof, making operator training a critical business consideration.

Can I finance the crane and truck separately or together?

Most lenders prefer integrated crane truck financing as a complete unit rather than separate truck and crane financing. This approach simplifies approval, provides better terms, and recognizes the equipment as a unified operational asset rather than separate components.

What maintenance considerations affect crane truck financing?

Crane trucks require both vehicle and crane maintenance, costing RM 20-35 per operating hour. Consider manufacturer service networks, parts availability, and certified technician access when selecting equipment brands. Many financing packages include extended warranties and service agreements.

How do I handle seasonal variations in crane truck demand?

Construction activity often follows seasonal patterns with peak demand during dry seasons. Build cash reserves during busy periods and consider diverse markets like telecommunications and utilities for year-round demand. Some lenders offer seasonal payment adjustments.

Should I choose new or used crane trucks for my first purchase?

New equipment offers warranties, latest technology, and easier financing but higher costs. Quality used equipment (3-5 years) provides better initial cash flow. Consider your budget, intended applications, and financing terms when deciding between new and used options.

What insurance coverage is specifically required for crane truck operations?

Beyond standard commercial vehicle insurance, you need crane operation liability, lifting coverage for goods being handled, public liability for job sites, and professional indemnity for crane services. Business interruption insurance is important given daily revenue potential.
