Frequently Asked Questions

What size road compactor should I finance for general construction work in Malaysia?

For general construction work, a 10-12 ton smooth drum roller provides excellent versatility for both soil and asphalt compaction. This size handles most residential and commercial projects while remaining economical to operate. Consider your typical project sizes and future growth when selecting capacity.

How do I qualify for government construction equipment financing programs?

Government programs typically require Malaysian citizenship, valid construction licenses (CIDB), proven project experience, and financial statements showing business viability. SME Bank requires 20% equity contribution, while TEKUN focuses on business potential rather than just financial strength.

What‚Äôs the typical payback period for a financed road compactor?

Most contractors achieve payback within 18-30 months through project work. Daily rental rates of RM 400-800 combined with project contracts provide steady revenue. Success depends on equipment utilization rates, project mix, and operational efficiency.

Can I finance multiple road compactors for fleet expansion?

Yes, many lenders offer fleet financing packages for multiple units. This approach often provides better terms than individual equipment loans and simplifies administration. Demonstrate strong project pipeline and management capability when applying for multiple equipment financing.

What maintenance considerations affect road compactor financing costs?

Factor maintenance costs of RM 15-25 per operating hour into your financial planning. Preventive maintenance programs can be included in financing packages. Consider manufacturer service agreements and local parts availability when selecting equipment brands and financing terms.

How do seasonal construction patterns affect payment schedules?

Many lenders offer seasonal payment adjustments recognizing construction industry cash flow patterns. Some programs allow reduced payments during monsoon seasons with higher payments during peak construction periods. Discuss seasonal flexibility during loan negotiations.

What happens if my road compactor is damaged on a construction site?

Comprehensive insurance should cover equipment damage, theft, and vandalism. Business interruption insurance can cover lost revenue during repair periods. Maintain detailed incident reports and coordinate closely with insurance companies for claim processing.

Should I choose new or used road compactors for my first purchase?

New equipment offers warranties, latest technology, and easier financing but higher costs. Quality used equipment (3-5 years) provides better initial cash flow with proven reliability. Consider your budget, project requirements, and financing terms when deciding between new and used options.
