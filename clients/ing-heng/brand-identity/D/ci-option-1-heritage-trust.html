Usage Guidelines

Do's ‚úÖ

  • Use Bronze Gold sparingly for emphasis and CTAs (5-10% of page)
  • Maintain generous white space for sophistication
  • Use logo octagon shape as decorative element
  • Pair serif headings with sans-serif body text
  • Use warm cream backgrounds to reduce eye strain
  • Incorporate subtle textures at 5% opacity

Don'ts ‚ùå

  • Don't use bright, vibrant colors (conflicts with heritage feel)
  • Don't overuse Bronze Gold (maintains exclusivity)
  • Don't use rounded corners beyond 8px
  • Don't mix more than 2 font families
  • Don't use gradients (too modern for this aesthetic)
  • Don't place logo on busy backgrounds

Why Choose This Option?

Best For

  • Established financial institutions wanting to emphasize longevity
  • B2B equipment financing in traditional industries (construction, manufacturing)
  • Clients who value stability over trendiness
  • Markets where heritage and trust are primary selling points
Immediately communicates trustworthiness and stability
Timeless design that won't feel dated in 5-10 years

Strong brand recognition through consistent Bronze Gold usage
Accessible color contrast ratios (WCAG AAA compliant)
Leverages existing logo perfectly

Considerations

  • May feel "old-fashioned" to younger entrepreneurs
  • Bronze Gold may not resonate with tech-forward industries
  • Less dynamic than modern, colorful alternatives
