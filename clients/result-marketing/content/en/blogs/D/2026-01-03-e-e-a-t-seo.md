## Demonstrating Expertise

**What is expertise?**
- Deep knowledge in your field
- Professional qualifications
- Specialized training

**How to demonstrate:**

### 1. Author Bios

**Create detailed author bios:**

**Include:**
- Full name (real person, not "Admin")
- Professional qualifications
- Years of experience
- Areas of specialization
- Education/certifications
- Photo

**Example:**
```
About the Author:
Ahmad Hassan is a certified construction equipment specialist
with 20+ years experience in crane rental and logistics. He
holds a Civil Engineering degree from UM and is a member of
the Master Builders Association Malaysia.
```

### 2. Qualifications Displayed

**Show credentials:**
- Certifications displayed on site
- Professional memberships
- Awards and recognition
- Licenses and permits

**Where to show:**
- About page
- Service pages
- Footer
- Author bios

### 3. Technical Content

**Demonstrate expertise through content:**
- Industry-specific terminology
- Technical details
- Insider knowledge
- Data and statistics
- Case studies with numbers

**Example:**
- Instead of: "We rent cranes"
- Use: "Our fleet includes 20-100 ton mobile cranes with
  lifting capacity up to 100 tons at 60-meter radius.
  All cranes are certified by Jabatan Pengangkutan Jalan."

**Shows: You know what you're talking about.**

### 4. Cite Sources

**Reference authoritative sources:**
- Industry associations
- Government regulations
- Research studies
- Manufacturer specifications

**Example:**
```
According to the Construction Industry Development Board
(CIDB), crane operators must be certified under the
Construction Personnel Certification Scheme.
```

**Shows: You're connected to industry standards.**

## Showing Experience

**What is experience?**
- Practical, hands-on knowledge
- Real-world application
- First-hand involvement

**How to demonstrate:**

### 1. Case Studies

**Detailed project examples:**

**Format:**
- Challenge (what problem)
- Solution (what you did)
- Results (specific outcomes)

**Example:**
```
Case Study: Terasek Supply Water Tanker Project

Challenge: Deliver 50 water tankers daily across Klang Valley
within 4 hours, while tracking GPS and maintaining
documentation.

Solution: Implemented digital paperwork system with
GPS tracking and automated scheduling.

Results:
- Delivery time reduced from 2 hours to 30 minutes
- Payment collection reduced from 60 days to 30 days
- Client satisfaction increased to 98%
```

**Shows: You've done this before (successfully).**

### 2. Project Portfolio

**Visual proof of experience:**
- Before/after photos
- Project gallery
- Client logos (with permission)
- Video testimonials

**Where to show:**
- Portfolio page
- Case studies page
- Service pages
- Homepage

### 3. Years in Business

**Prominently display:**
- "Since 2015"
- "20+ years experience"
- "500+ projects completed"

**Social proof of experience.**

### 4. Team Experience

**Show your team:**
- Team photos
- Individual bios
- Years of service
- Areas of expertise

**Example:**
```
Our Team:

Ahmad - Founder, 20 years construction experience
Siti - Operations Manager, 15 years logistics
Kumar - Technical Lead, 18 years heavy equipment
```

**Shows: Deep collective experience.**

## Building Authoritativeness

**What is authoritativeness?**
- Recognition as expert in your field
- Citations by others
- Media mentions
- Industry leadership

**How to build:**

### 1. Backlinks from Authoritative Sites

**Quality > quantity:**
- Industry publications
- News sites (The Star, Malay Mail)
- Government sites (.gov.my)
- Educational sites (.edu.my)
- Major industry associations

**Example:**
- Featured in "Construction Malaysia" magazine
- Quoted in NST Business Times
- Listed on MBAM website

### 2. Media Coverage

**Get press coverage:**
- Press releases for newsworthy events
- Offer expert quotes to journalists
- Sponsor industry events
- Conduct original research (survey results)

### 3. Guest Posting

**Write for authoritative sites:**
- Industry blogs
- Business publications
- Professional associations

**Benefit:**
- Exposure to new audience
- Author bio with link
- Builds authority

### 4. Social Proof

**Show recognition:**
- Awards and certifications
- Client testimonials (with photos)
- Partner logos
- Industry memberships
- "As seen in" media logos

### 5. Wikipedia/Industry Wikis

**Get listed (if notable):**
- Company Wikipedia page
- Industry directory listings
- Who's Who listings

## Establishing Trustworthiness

**What is trustworthiness?**
- Credibility and reliability
- Transparency and honesty
- Security and safety

**How to establish:**

### 1. Contact Information Prominent

**Show clearly:**
- Physical address (not just P.O. Box)
- Phone number (answered by humans)
- Email (professional domain, not @gmail.com)
- Business hours
- WhatsApp (preferred in Malaysia)

**Why:** Transparency builds trust.

### 2. About Us Page

**Detailed information:**
- Company history
- Team bios
- Mission/values
- Behind-the-scenes
- Photos of real people/office

**Shows: Real business, real people.**

### 3. Reviews and Testimonials

**Display prominently:**
- Google reviews (embed widget)
- Client testimonials (with photos, names)
- Case studies (detailed success stories)
- Video testimonials

**Real reviews from real clients = trust.**

### 4. Security Signals

**Show your site is secure:**
- SSL certificate (HTTPS)
- Privacy policy
- Terms of service
- Secure payment (if e-commerce)
- Data protection statement

### 5. Accurate Business Information

**NAP consistency:**
- Name, address, phone consistent everywhere
- Accurate on all platforms
- Up-to-date information

**Inconsistent info = untrustworthy.**

### 6. Content Quality

**High-quality content = trust:**
- No spelling/grammar errors
- Well-researched, accurate
- Regularly updated
- Not clickbait or misleading
- Cites sources

### 7. Professional Design

**Looks trustworthy:**
- Clean, modern design
- Mobile-responsive
- Fast loading
- No broken links
- No annoying pop-ups

## FAQ

**Q: How long does it take to build E-E-A-T?**
A: Ongoing process. Start with foundations (bios, testimonials, accurate info). Build authority over months/years.

**Q: Is E-E-A-T a ranking factor?**
A: Directly? No (not in algorithm). Indirectly? Yes (Quality Raters use E-E-A-T to evaluate sites, feedback influences algorithm).

**Q: What's most important for small businesses?**
A: Focus on Trustworthiness first (accurate info, contact details, reviews). Then Expertise (author bios, quality content).
