## How to Find Keywords

### Tool 1: Google Keyword Planner (Free)

**Steps:**
1. Go to ads.google.com â†’ Tools â†’ Keyword Planner
2. Enter seed keywords (your service + location)
3. Filter by Malaysia
4. Sort by "Avg. monthly searches"

**What to look for:**
- 100-1,000 searches/month (sweet spot)
- Low competition (easier to rank)
- High CPC (indicates commercial value)

### Tool 2: Google Suggestions (Free)

**Type in Google:**
- Your service + location
- Watch auto-suggestions

**Examples:**
- Type "crane rental"
- Google suggests:
  - "crane rental near me"
  - "crane rental price"
  - "crane rental kuala lumpur"
  - "mobile crane rental"

**These are real searches people make.**

### Tool 3: Google "People Also Ask" (Free)

**Search your keyword**
- Scroll to "People Also Ask" section
- Click questions to expand more

**These are:**
- Real user questions
- Content ideas
- Long-tail keyword opportunities

### Tool 4: Competitor Analysis (Free)

**Steps:**
1. Search your target keyword
2. Open top 3 competitors
3. View page source
4. Search for "keyword"
5. See what they're targeting

**Steal their keyword strategy.**

### Tool 5: Ahrefs/SEMrush (Paid)

**Features:**
- All keywords competitors rank for
- Keyword difficulty scores
- Search volume trends
- Click-through data

**Worth it if:**
- Competitive industry
- Budget available
- Need comprehensive data

## Analyzing Keyword Value

### 3 Factors to Evaluate:

**1. Search Volume**
- 10-100/month: Niche (easy to rank, limited traffic)
- 100-1,000/month: Sweet spot (good traffic, reasonable competition)
- 1,000-10,000/month: Competitive (hard to rank, lots of traffic)
- 10,000+/month: Very competitive (only for established sites)

**2. Keyword Difficulty**
- 0-10: Easy (new sites can rank)
- 11-20: Moderate (some SEO effort)
- 21-30: Hard (requires authority)
- 31+: Very hard (only for established sites)

**3. Commercial Intent**
- High: "near me", "price", "rental", "hire"
- Medium: "best", "top", "reviews"
- Low: "how to", "what is", "guide"

**Perfect keyword:**
- 100-1,000 searches/month
- Difficulty 0-20
- High commercial intent

## FAQ

**Q: How many keywords should I target?**
A: Start with 20-50. Focus on ranking for these, then expand. Quality > quantity.

**Q: Should I target high-volume keywords?**
A: Only if you have authority. New sites: Start with low competition (100-1,000 searches). Build up to high volume.

**Q: What's keyword difficulty?**
A: Score (0-100) estimating how hard to rank. 0-20 = easy for new sites. 31+ = requires established authority.

**Q: Can I rank for multiple keywords on one page?**
A: Yes. One page can rank for 5-20 related keywords. Focus on primary keyword, secondary keywords will follow.
