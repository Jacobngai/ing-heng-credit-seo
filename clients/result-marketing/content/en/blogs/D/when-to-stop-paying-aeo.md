## **Direct Answer: When to Reduce AEO Investment**

Scale AEO investment down when you reach 1,200+ AI Overview citations and 50+ domain authority, shifting to maintenance mode (RM500-800/month instead of RM1,500-2,500). Never stop completely - AI citations decay within 60-90 days without fresh content and schema updates. Key indicators: citation growth plateaus for 2+ months, ROI exceeds 1,000% consistently, and you dominate target queries (60%+ share of voice). Maintain spend if you're still gaining citations, targeting new query categories, or competitors are increasing AEO activity.

## [Signs to Scale Down](#signs-to-scale-down)

### 1. Citation Saturation

**You've hit peak citations when:**
- 1,200+ AI Overview citations across target queries
- 60%+ share of voice for core keywords
- Citation growth < 5% month-over-month for 2+ months

**Action:** Reduce AEO spend by 40-60%. Shift to maintenance.

**Example:**
- Current investment: RM2,000/month
- Citations: 1,450 (saturated)
- Share of voice: 68%
- **New investment: RM800/month (maintenance)**

---

### 2. Domain Authority Plateau

**You've maxed out link equity when:**
- Domain authority 50+ (Ahrefs/Moz)
- No increase for 3+ months despite link building
- Ranking #1-3 for target keywords anyway

**Action:** Pivot budget from link building to content expansion.

**Example:**
- Old split: 40% links, 60% content = RM2,000/month
- New split: 10% links, 90% content = RM1,200/month
- **Savings: RM800/month**

### 3. Query Dominance

**You own the queries when:**
- Position 1 AI citations for 80%+ of target queries
- Branded search up 200%+ from baseline
- Competitors rarely appear in your AI answers

**Action:** Expand to new query categories, don't over-invest in existing ones.

**Example:**
- Phase 1: "excavator rental" queries (dominated)
- Phase 2: "bulldozer rental" queries (new investment)
- Phase 3: "crane rental" queries (new investment)

**Result:** Constant growth without increasing monthly budget.

### 4. ROI Above 1,000%

**Exceptional returns mean:**
- You're generating 10x+ revenue vs investment
- Cost per acquisition under RM200
- Lead quality is consistently high

**Action:** Maintain current investment. Don't increase unless scaling to new markets/locations.

**Example:**
- Investment: RM1,500/month
- Revenue: RM180,000/month (12,000% ROI)
- **Decision:** Keep RM1,500, reinvest profits into business growth

---

### 5. Market Share Cap

**Local businesses hit limits:**
- You serve 1-3 cities maximum
- 80%+ of local customers know you
- You're at capacity (can't handle more leads)

**Action:** Maintenance mode only.

**Example:**

## [Signs to Maintain Investment](#signs-to-maintain-investment)

- New queries triggering AI Overviews in your niche

**Example:**
- Month 5: 850 citations
- Month 6: 1,050 citations (23% growth)
- **Decision:** Maintain RM1,800/month investment

## **FAQ: AEO Investment Optimization**

### **Can I really stop AEO completely after 6 months?**

No. Citations decay 50% within 60-90 days without maintenance. Competitors will overtake your positions. Scale down to RM500-800/month maintenance mode, never stop completely.

### **What's the minimum viable AEO maintenance budget?**

RM500/month for single-location local businesses. RM1,000/month for multi-location. This covers citation monitoring, 2-4 content updates/month, and schema maintenance. Below RM500/month, you'll lose positions.

### **How do I know if I've hit citation saturation?**

Three indicators: (1) Citation growth <5% for 2+ months, (2) 60%+ share of voice for core queries, (3) You appear in 80%+ of AI Overviews for target keywords. At this point, shift to maintenance mode.

### **Should I increase AEO spend if ROI is good?**

Only if expanding to new locations, services, or query categories. If you're saturated in existing areas, increasing spend has diminishing returns. Better to pivot to new opportunities than overspend on saturated ones.

### **What's the typical AEO investment lifecycle?**

Growth phase: RM1,500-2,500/month for 4-6 months. Transition phase: RM1,000-1,500/month for 2 months. Maintenance phase: RM500-800/month ongoing. Total first-year cost: RM14,400-18,000. Typical first-year revenue: RM500,000-1,200,000.
