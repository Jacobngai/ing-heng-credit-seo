## **When AEO Actually Stops Working**

### Scenario 1: You've Hit Saturation

**Example:**
- You rank #1 for all 50 target keywords
- AI Overviews cite you for 80% of queries
- Market share: 35% (realistic ceiling for most local businesses)

**Month 13-18:**
- New leads: RM20,000/month (stable)
- AEO cost: RM1,200/month
- ROI: 16.7x

**Verdict:** Keep paying. RM1,200 for RM20,000 is a no-brainer.

**But if:**
- New leads drop to RM2,000/month
- AEO cost: RM1,200/month
- ROI: 1.7x

**Verdict:** Stop or renegotiate.

### Scenario 2: You've Built In-House Capability

**Month 1-12:** Agency handles everything
- Strategy
- Content
- Technical optimization
- Schema markup
- Reporting

**Month 12+:** Your team can now:
- Publish content without agency help
- Implement schema changes
- Track AI Overview appearances
- Adjust strategy based on data

**Verdict:** Switch to maintenance mode (RM500/month) or bring fully in-house.

### Scenario 3: Your Business Changed

**Example:** You started as "KL crane rental" and pivoted to "Malaysia-wide construction equipment leasing."

Old AEO work:
- Focused on "crane rental KL"
- Local business schema
- Location-specific content

New reality:
- Needs national SEO strategy
- Different keyword clusters
- New schema markup

**Verdict:** Stop old AEO contract. Start new one with updated strategy.

## **The Compound Effect Trap**

Here's why most businesses exit too early:

**Scenario A: Exit at Month 6 (Premature)**
- Total investment: RM7,200
- Month 6 revenue: RM5,000
- Net result: -RM2,200 (loss)
- **Verdict:** "SEO doesn't work"

**Scenario B: Exit at Month 18 (Smart)**
- Total investment: RM21,600
- Month 6-18 revenue: RM120,000
- Net result: +RM98,400 (profit)
- **Verdict:** "SEO prints money"

**The difference:** Compound effects.

AEO builds on itself. Month 1 content supports Month 12 rankings. Stop at Month 6, and you kill the compounding before it pays off.

**Rule of thumb:** Commit to 12 months minimum. 18 months is better.
