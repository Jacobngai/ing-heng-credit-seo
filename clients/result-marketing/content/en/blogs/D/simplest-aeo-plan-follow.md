**Key Takeaways:**
- Step 1: Add FAQ schema to 5 key pages (2 hours, one-time)
- Step 2: Create 3 question-targeted posts monthly (3 hours)
- Step 3: Monitor 50 core queries weekly (1 hour)
- Step 4: Track branded search growth (30 min monthly)

**Read Time:** 5 minutes | **Category:** DIY vs Hire

## **Direct Answer: Simplest AEO Framework**

The simplest effective AEO plan has 4 steps: (1) Add FAQ schema with 10-15 questions to your top 5 service pages, (2) Publish 3 question-targeted articles monthly (how-to, pricing, comparison), (3) Manually check 50 core queries for AI Overview citations weekly, (4) Monitor branded search growth in Google Search Console. This 4-5 hour monthly routine delivers 60-70% of agency results within 6 months. Focus on queries already triggering AI Overviews, optimize for citation format (tables, lists, Q&A), and maintain consistency.

---

## [Step 1: FAQ Schema Setup](#step-1-faq-schema-setup)

### One-Time Setup (2 Hours)

**What to do:**
1. Install RankMath or Yoast SEO plugin (free tier works)
2. Navigate to your top 5 service pages
3. Add FAQ schema with 10-15 questions each
4. Test with Google's Rich Results Test

---

### FAQ Template That Works

**For Each Service Page:**

**Pricing Questions (3-4):**
- How much does [service] cost?
- What factors affect [service] pricing?
- Do you offer free estimates for [service]?
- Are there hidden fees for [service]?

**Process Questions (3-4):**
- How long does [service] take?
- What's the process for [service]?
- Do I need to prepare anything before [service]?
- What happens after [service] is complete?

**Quality Questions (3-4):**
- Are you certified for [service]?
- How do you ensure quality for [service]?
- What makes your [service] different?
- Can you show examples of [service]?

**Location Questions (2-3):**
- Do you offer [service] in [my area]?
- How far do you travel for [service]?
- Is [service] available in [neighboring cities]?

## [Step 2: Question-Targeted Content](#step-2-question-targeted-content)

### Monthly Content Plan (3 Hours/Month)

**Article 1: "How Much" Article**
- Target: "How much does [service] cost in [city]?"
- Length: 800-1,000 words
- Include: Price ranges, factors, comparison table

**Article 2: "How To" Article**
- Target: "How to [achieve outcome] with [service]"
- Length: 1,000-1,200 words
- Include: Step-by-step process, timeline, preparation

**Article 3: Comparison Article**
- Target: "[Your service] vs [alternative]"
- Length: 800-1,000 words
- Include: Pros/cons table, when to choose each

---

## **FAQ: Simple AEO Plan**

### **Is this really enough to compete with agencies?**

Yes, for 60-70% of agency results. Agencies add: advanced schema (Article, Organization), faster content velocity (6-8 posts/month), professional tools (Semrush, Surfer SEO), and experience. But this simple plan delivers the core 80/20: FAQ schema, question-targeted content, citation monitoring.

### **What if I miss a week of monitoring?**

It's fine. Weekly monitoring is ideal, but bi-weekly still works. The key is consistency over intensity. Missing one week won't kill your results. Missing 3-4 months will. Build a sustainable routine you can maintain long-term.

### **Can I do just 1-2 articles per month instead of 3?**

Yes, but results will be slower. 3 articles/month = 800-1,000 citations in 6 months. 2 articles/month = 500-700 citations. 1 article/month = 300-400 citations. More content velocity = faster citation growth. Do what you can sustain.

### **How do I find the 50 queries to monitor?**

Start with Google Search Console. Look at your current queries. Add "how much", "how to", "near me" modifiers. Check AnswerThePublic (free tier) for question ideas. Ask your sales team: "What questions do customers ask before buying?" You'll have 50+ quickly.

### **What's the minimum viable version of this plan?**

Absolute minimum: (1) Add FAQ schema to 3 pages (1 hour, one-time), (2) Create 2 question-targeted posts monthly (2 hours), (3) Monitor 20 core queries bi-weekly (1 hour). Total: 3 hours/month. You'll get 40-50% of agency results. Better than nothing.
