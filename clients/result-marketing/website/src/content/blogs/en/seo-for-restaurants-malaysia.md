## Step 1: Fix Your Google Business Profile (Foundation)

Your GBP isn't just "another directory listing." It's the single most important ranking factor for local restaurants. Here's how we optimize it:

### 1.1 Nail Your Primary Category

**The Mistake:** Choosing "Restaurant" or "Cafe" because it's generic and covers everything.
**The Fix:** Google uses category to match search intent. Be specific.

| Generic (Bad) | Specific (Good) | When to Use |
|---------------|-----------------|-------------|
| Restaurant | Malaysian Restaurant | Serve local cuisine? |
| Cafe | Breakfast Restaurant | Strong breakfast crowd? |
| Restaurant | Halal Restaurant | Halal certified? |
| Restaurant | Family Restaurant | Kid-friendly environment? |
| Cafe | Coffee Shop | Specialty coffee focus? |

**Real Example:** We changed a PJ cafe from "Cafe" to "Breakfast Restaurant" because their best-selling items were breakfast sets. Their impression share for "breakfast PJ" jumped from 5% to 42% in 3 weeks.

### 1.2 Define Your Service Area Correctly

**Situation A: You have a physical restaurant**
- Show your actual address
- Enable "Customers visit my business"
- Add your service area (5-10km radius max ‚Äî don't claim the whole Klang Valley)

**Situation B: Delivery/Cloud Kitchen**
- Hide your address
- Set precise delivery zones (postcodes or neighborhoods)
- Be specific about delivery platforms (GrabFood, Foodpanda, ShopeeFood)

**Why It Matters:** Google displays restaurants based on proximity to the searcher. If you're claiming to serve KL but you're actually in Shah Alam, Google knows. They'll deprioritize you for KL searches.

### 1.3 Complete Every Field (And Mean It)

**The fields Google uses:**

| Field | What to Put | What to Avoid |
|-------|-------------|---------------|
| Business Name | Actual name (no keywords) | "Best Nasi Lemak KL Restaurant" |
| Description | 750 characters, natural language | Keyword stuffing |
| Opening Hours | Accurate hours (update for holidays) | "Open daily" ‚Äî Google wants specifics |
| Phone Number | Direct line (no extensions) | Main office line if different |
| Website | Home page or booking page | Broken redirects or 404s |
| Attributes | Outdoor seating, WiFi, Halal, etc. | Leaving this blank |

**Pro Tip:** Use the "Special hours" feature for public holidays. Malaysians plan meals around CNY, Hari Raya, Deepavali. Show you're open when they're searching.

## Step 2: Build a Review Engine (Social Proof = Rankings)

Here's something most restaurants miss: **Google doesn't just count reviews. They measure velocity and engagement.**

What matters:
- **Quantity:** Aim for 100+ reviews minimum (top performers have 500+)
- **Recency:** Reviews from the last 30 days matter most
- **Response Rate:** Responding to 100% of reviews signals active management
- **Rating:** 4.6+ is the sweet spot (5.0 looks fake to some users)

### Our 3-Step Review System

**Step 1: Make It Easy**
Don't make customers hunt for your review link. Generate a QR code that goes directly to your Google review page. Put it on:
- Table tents
- Receipts
- Menu cover
- Counter signage

**Step 2: Train Your Staff**
The moment you say "please review us on Google," customers feel pressured. Here's what works:
- "Thanks for dining with us! We'd love to hear how it went. There's a QR code on your receipt if you have a minute."
- Natural, low-pressure, helpful.

**Step 3: Respond to Every Review**
Every single one. Good reviews deserve thanks. Bad reviews deserve explanations.

**Good response example:**
> "Thank you so much for the kind words, Sarah! We're thrilled you enjoyed the nasi lemak. See you again soon! üëã"

**Bad response (and we see this too often):**
> "Thanks." (One word. Low effort.)

**Data Point:** Restaurants that respond to 100% of reviews get 27% more profile views than those that don't (BrightLocal, 2025). The numbers don't lie.

## Step 3: Optimize Your Website's Menu Pages

Your menu isn't just a PDF that customers download. It's a goldmine of SEO keywords that Google is desperate to understand.

### 3.1 What Google Looks For

When someone searches "chicken rice KL," Google wants to show:
1. Restaurants that serve chicken rice
2. In the KL area
3. With good ratings and recent reviews
4. With menus that mention chicken rice

Your menu page is proof you serve what people are searching for.

### 3.2 Menu Page SEO Checklist

| Element | Optimization |
|---------|--------------|
| Page Title | "Menu - [Restaurant Name] | [Specialty]" |
| Meta Description | "Authentic [specialty] in [area]. Try our [signature dishes]. Book now or order via [delivery platform]." |
| H1 | "Our Menu" or "Food Menu" |
| Category Headings | "Chicken Rice," "Nasi Lemak," "Beverages" ‚Äî not "Section A," "Section B" |
| Dish Descriptions | 2-3 sentences per dish. Include ingredients, preparation style, what makes it special. |
| Prices | Display clearly. Google uses structured data for rich snippets. |
| Dietary Info | Halal, vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free options. |

**Example of good menu SEO:**

### Hainanese Chicken Rice

Our signature dish. Poached chicken served with fragrant jasmine rice, cucumber slices, and three sauces. The rice is cooked with chicken fat and pandan leaves for authentic aroma. Choose from:
- White (steamed)
- Roasted
- Soy sauce chicken

RM 14.00

**What this tells Google:**
- Keyword: "hainanese chicken rice"
- Location: Context implies Malaysia (based on pricing and description style)
- Detail: Rich description = better understanding

### 3.3 Schema Markup (The Technical Edge)

Add structured data to your menu pages so Google can display them in search results:

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "Menu",
  "name": "Restaurant Name Menu",
  "description": "Authentic Malaysian cuisine in KL",
  "hasMenuSection": [
    {
      "@type": "MenuSection",
      "name": "Chicken Rice",
      "hasMenuItem": {
        "@type": "MenuItem",
        "name": "Hainanese Chicken Rice",
        "description": "Poached chicken with fragrant rice",
        "offers": {
          "@type": "Offer",
          "price": "14.00",
          "priceCurrency": "MYR"
        }
      }
    }
  ]
}
```

**Result:** Google displays your menu items directly in search results. Click-through rate increases by 30-40%.

Your restaurant name should be EXACTLY the same everywhere:
