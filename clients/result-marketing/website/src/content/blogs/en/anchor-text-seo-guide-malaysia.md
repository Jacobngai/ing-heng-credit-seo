## What Is Anchor Text (And Why Google Cares)

Anchor text is the clickable words in a hyperlink. Like this:

**Bad:** Learn more about [SEO services](link)  
**Good:** Discover [Malaysia SEO strategies that actually work](link)

Google uses anchor text as a **relevance signal**. When 50 websites link to your "equipment financing" page using the anchor "business loans Malaysia," Google assumes your page is about business loans — not equipment financing.

This is why anchor text matters: **it tells Google what your page is about**.

But here's the catch: Over-optimization triggers penalties. Use exact-match anchors too much, and Google thinks you're manipulating rankings.

The strategy isn't "use keywords everywhere." It's **natural diversity that signals relevance without looking artificial**.

## The 5 Anchor Text Types (And When to Use Each)

After analyzing 200+ penalty cases and successful campaigns, we categorize anchor text into 5 types. **A healthy link profile uses all 5 in the right ratios**.

### 1. Exact Match (Use Sparingly)

**Example:** "equipment financing Malaysia"

**When to use:**  
- High-authority editorial links (news sites, industry publications)  
- Guest posts on relevant blogs (maximum 1 per article)  
- Directory citations (business name + keyword)

**Why it's risky:**  
Google's Penguin algorithm specifically targets sites with unnatural exact-match ratios. Use this **less than 15% of the time**.

**Real case:** Logistics company in Johor used exact-match anchors for 40% of backlinks. Traffic dropped 60% overnight after Penguin update. Took 8 months to recover with anchor text diversification.

### 2. Partial Match (Your Go-To Anchor)

**Example:** "best practices for SEO in Malaysia"  
(Target keyword: Malaysia SEO)

**When to use:**  
- Guest posts (primary anchor type)  
- Content partnerships  
- Resource page links  
- Outreach campaigns

**Why it works:**  
Contains your keyword but reads naturally. **Aim for 40-50% partial match** in your anchor profile.

**Pro tip:** Combine keyword with modifiers:
- "comprehensive guide to [keyword]"
- "how to implement [keyword]"
- "common mistakes with [keyword]"

### 3. Branded (Safe & Powerful)

**Example:** "Result Marketing" or "according to Result Marketing"

**When to use:**  
- Every opportunity where exact/partial match doesn't fit  
- Social profiles  
- Citations and mentions  
- Author bios

**Why it works:**  
**Zero penalty risk**. Google expects branded anchors. Large brands have 40-60% branded anchor profiles.

**Target ratio:** 30-40% of total backlinks.

### 4. Generic (Necessary for Naturalness)

**Example:** "click here," "read more," "this article," "check it out"

**When to use:**  
- Social media shares  
- User-generated content  
- Forum signatures (if allowed)  
- Navigation links

**Why it matters:**  
**Real users write generic anchors**. A profile with zero generic anchors looks artificial.

**Target ratio:** 10-20%.

### 5. Naked URLs (Trust Signals)

**Example:** https://resultmarketing.asia

**When to use:**  
- Citations  
- Press releases  
- Social profiles  
- Resource lists

**Why it works:**  
Shows you're not trying to manipulate. **Natural link profiles always include naked URLs**.

**Target ratio:** 5-10%.

## The 3 Fatal Anchor Text Mistakes (And How to Fix Them)

### Mistake #1: "Click Here" Syndrome

**What we see:**  
"For more information about our services, [click here](link)."

**Why it fails:**  
Zero relevance signal. Google learns nothing about your page.

**The fix:**  
Rewrite the sentence so the keyword phrase becomes the natural link target.

**Better:**  
"Discover our [Malaysia SEO services for manufacturing companies](link)."

**Impact:** One logistics client rewrote 30 internal links from generic to partial-match. **Organic traffic increased 24% in 60 days** (no other changes).

### Mistake #2: Over-Optimization (The Penguin Killer)

**What we see:**  
Every backlink uses the exact keyword "equipment financing Malaysia."

**Why it fails:**  
Google's algorithm assumes manipulation. **Unnatural patterns = penalty risk**.

**The fix:**  
Diversify immediately. Use our distribution table above.

**How to audit:**  
1. Export backlinks from Ahrefs/SEMrush  
2. Count anchor text types  
3. Calculate percentages  
4. Rebalance future link building

**Recovery story:** Construction firm had 70% exact-match anchors. We built 50 branded + generic links over 4 months. Rankings recovered from page 4 to page 1 for primary keywords.

### Mistake #3: Internal Link Neglect

**What we see:**  
Malaysian businesses obsess over backlinks but ignore internal anchor text.

**Why it matters:**  
**Internal links are 100% under your control**. They guide Google's understanding of your site hierarchy.

**The fix:**  
Audit your top 20 pages. For each:
- Identify the primary keyword  
- Find 3-5 relevant pages to link from  
- Use partial-match anchors naturally in content

**Best practice:**  
Link from high-authority pages (homepage, top blog posts) to pages you want to rank.

**Case study:** eCommerce site added 40 strategic internal links over 2 weeks. **Target pages jumped an average of 8 positions** without any external link building.

## How to Build Links with Proper Anchor Text (Malaysia Strategy)

Most Malaysian businesses think link building is begging for backlinks. That's why they fail.

**Here's our proven process:**

### Step 1: Map Your Target Keywords to Pages

Create a spreadsheet:

| Page | Primary Keyword | Partial-Match Variations |
|------|-----------------|--------------------------|
| /services/seo | Malaysia SEO | SEO services Malaysia, SEO strategies for Malaysian businesses |
| /blog/local-seo | local SEO guide | how to do local SEO in Malaysia, local search optimization tips |

### Step 2: Create Link-Worthy Content

**Not all pages deserve links.** Google rewards pages that provide unique value.

**What works in Malaysia:**
- Original research (survey 100 local businesses, publish findings)  
- Comprehensive guides (10x longer than competitors)  
- Free tools (ROI calculators, audit checkers)  
- Industry reports (quarterly trend analysis)

**Example:** Our "SEO Pricing Malaysia" guide gets 12 backlinks monthly because it's the **only source with real pricing data** (we surveyed 30 agencies).

### Step 3: Outreach with Anchor Text Suggestions

When requesting a link, **don't leave anchor text to chance**.

**Template:**  
"Hi [Name],

I noticed your article on [topic]. Great insights on [specific detail].

I recently published a [comprehensive guide/study/tool] on [related topic] that your readers might find valuable: [link]

If you think it's a good fit, you could reference it in your section on [specific section]. Something like:

**Suggested anchor:** '[partial-match anchor text]' ([link])

Let me know if you'd like me to return the favor!

Best,  
[Your Name]"

**Why this works:**  
You're making it easy for them. Most people will use your suggested anchor.

### Step 4: Monitor and Rebalance

Every quarter:
1. Export backlinks from Ahrefs  
2. Calculate anchor text distribution  
3. Adjust future link building to maintain ratios

**Tool recommendation:** Ahrefs Anchor Text Report (best for Malaysian sites)

## Advanced Tactics: LSI & Co-Occurrence

Google doesn't just look at anchor text. It looks at **words surrounding the link**.

**Co-occurrence example:**  
If a link to your "SEO services" page is surrounded by phrases like "Malaysia digital marketing," "search engine optimization," and "website ranking," Google understands the context even if the anchor is generic ("click here").

**How to use this:**  
When doing guest posts or outreach, ensure the **paragraph around your link includes relevant keywords**.

**Before (weak):**  
"We found this helpful resource. [Click here](link) to read more."

**After (strong):**  
"When implementing **Malaysia SEO strategies** for local businesses, understanding technical optimization is critical. This [comprehensive SEO guide](link) covers everything from **schema markup** to **local citations**."

Notice: Even with a partial-match anchor, we reinforced relevance with surrounding keywords.
