## Design Style

### Visual Language
**Core Principles:**
- **Maximum Contrast** - Black and white with purple pops
- **Bold Typography** - Heavy weights, large sizes, tight tracking
- **Disruptive Messaging** - Challenge conventions, punchy copy
- **Unapologetic** - Stand out, don't blend in

**Key Characteristics:**
- Dark backgrounds with bright accents
- Ultra-bold headings (800-900 weight)
- Sharp corners or minimal rounding (2-4px)
- High-energy CTAs with vibrant colors
- Anti-design elements (asymmetry, raw layouts)

## Usage Guidelines

### Do's
✅ Use black backgrounds with bright accents
✅ Go bold with typography (800-900 weights)
✅ Use maximum contrast (black/white/purple)
✅ Make CTAs massive and impossible to miss
✅ Use disruptive, punchy messaging
✅ Add subtle animations (glows, pulses, floats)
✅ Embrace asymmetry and anti-design elements

### Don'ts
❌ Don't use more than 3 accent colors (purple + pink + green)
❌ Don't make text smaller than 15px for body
❌ Don't use rounded corners larger than 12px
❌ Don't be afraid of negative space (bold needs breathing room)
❌ Don't water down the contrast (keep it high)
❌ Don't use subtle shadows (go big or go home)
❌ Don't blend in - stand out

## Why Choose This Option

### Best For
- **Disruptive brands** challenging industry norms
- **Competitive markets** where standing out is critical
- **Young, bold audiences** tired of corporate B2B
- **Brands willing to polarize** (love it or hate it - no middle ground)

### Strengths
✅ **Impossible to ignore** - High contrast demands attention
✅ **Memorable** - People won't forget this brand
✅ **Differentiates** - Stands out from conservative competitors
✅ **Energy** - Feels dynamic, exciting, urgent
✅ **Bold positioning** - Clearly communicates "we're different"

### Potential Weaknesses
⚠️ May alienate conservative clients (construction, finance)
⚠️ Can feel aggressive or "too much" for some audiences
⚠️ Risk of looking dated if trends shift
⚠️ Requires confidence to pull off (not for everyone)
