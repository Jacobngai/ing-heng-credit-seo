**The Superhost badge.**

## Part 1: The 4 Superhost Criteria

### Criterion #1: 4.8+ Overall Rating

**Requirement:** Maintain a 4.8 overall rating from guests

**What This Means:**
- Your average across all review categories must be 4.8 or higher
- One 3-star review won't kill it, but consistent 4-star reviews will
- 5-star reviews should be your norm (80%+ of all reviews)

---

**The 5 Review Categories:**

| Category | What Guests Rate | Target Score |
|----------|-----------------|--------------|
| **Overall Experience** | General satisfaction | 4.8+ |
| **Cleanliness** | How clean was the unit? | 4.8+ |
| **Accuracy** | Did listing match reality? | 4.8+ |
| **Communication** | How responsive were you? | 4.8+ |
| **Location** | Is the location as described? | 4.8+ |
| **Check-in** | Was arrival smooth? | 4.8+ |

**Note:** Location is often the lowest score. You can't change your location, but you CAN manage expectations in your listing description.

---

**What Malaysian Guests Care About Most:**

**Top Complaints That Kill Ratings:**
1. **Cleanliness** (" dusty," "hair in bathroom," "smells musty")
2. **WiFi Speed** ("couldn't work," "streaming was slow")
3. **Comfort** ("bed was hard," "pillows were flat")
4. **Noise** ("could hear neighbors," "traffic noise")
5. **Check-In Issues** ("couldn't find parking," "key didn't work")

**Fix These First:** Invest in quality mattresses, professional cleaning, fast WiFi, clear check-in instructions.

---

### Criterion #2: 10+ Stays (or 3 reservations for 100+ nights)

**Requirement:** Hosted 10+ stays OR completed 3 reservations totaling 100+ nights

**What This Means:**
- New hosts need 10 separate bookings to qualify
- One guest staying for 30 nights counts as 1 stay
- Ten separate guests staying 2 nights each = 10 stays
- Assessment happens every 3 months (quarterly)

**Timeline to Qualify:**
- Fast: 2-3 months (high demand area, well-priced)
- Typical: 3-6 months
- Slow: 6-12 months (new listing, competitive market)

---

### Criterion #3: <1% Cancellation Rate

**Requirement:** Cancel less than 1% of reservations

**What This Means:**
- Cancel 1 booking out of 100+ = maintain Superhost
- Cancel 1 booking out of 50 = lose Superhost
- Extenuating circumstances don't count against you

**What Counts as Cancellation:**
- You cancel (for any reason)
- You decline a booking request (counts as cancellation)
- Guest cancels = does NOT count against you

---

**How to Maintain <1% Cancellation:**

**1. Use Calendar Sync**
- List on multiple platforms? Sync calendars automatically
- Prevent double-bookings (main reason for cancellations)

**2. Don't Overbook**
- Block dates when you're unavailable
- Update calendar immediately when booking comes in

**3. Use Instant Book Carefully**
- Ensure you can accommodate all booking types
- Set house rules clearly
- Verify ID requirements

**4. Communicate Before Booking**
- Answer inquiries quickly
- Clarify any guest questions BEFORE they book
- Ensure guest understands listing (avoid disappointment cancellations)

---

### Criterion #4: 90%+ Response Rate Within 24 Hours

**Requirement:** Respond to 90% of new messages within 24 hours

**What This Means:**
- Out of 100 messages, you must respond to 90+ within 24 hours
- 10 messages can take longer without penalty
- But why would you want any guest to wait 24+ hours?

**Best Practice:** Respond within 1 hour. Guests book quickly. First responder wins.

---

**What Counts as a Response:**
- Reply to inquiry
- Reply to booking request
- Pre-arrival message
- During-stay message
- Post-stay message

**What Doesn't Count:**
- Guest messages after checkout (thank you notes)
- Reviews
- Resolution center messages

## Part 2: How Superhost Assessment Works

### Assessment Schedule

**Every Quarter (January 1, April 1, July 1, October 1):**

1. **Performance Period:** Previous 4 quarters (365 days)
2. **Evaluation:** Airbnb checks your stats against all 4 criteria
3. **Result:** Superhost status awarded, maintained, or lost

---

**Timeline Examples:**

**New Host:**
- January 1: Start hosting
- April 1: First assessment (need 10+ stays)
- July 1: First chance to qualify (if all criteria met)

**Existing Host:**
- Maintained Superhost: Status continues
- Missed criteria: Status lost immediately
- Can re-qualify next assessment (3 months later)

---

### Status Loss & Recovery

**Losing Superhost:**
- Happens immediately upon assessment failure
- Badge removed from profile
- Search ranking drops
- Bookings may decrease

**Recovering Superhost:**
- Wait for next assessment (3 months)
- Meet all 4 criteria during that period
- Status restored automatically

**Impact of Losing Status:**
- 10-22% drop in bookings (temporary)
- Search ranking lower until restored
- Some guests filter for Superhosts only
