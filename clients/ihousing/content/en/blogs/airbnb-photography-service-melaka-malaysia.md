## What Makes Great Airbnb Photography?

### Technical Excellence

**1. Proper Lighting**

**Amateur mistake:** Using flash or shooting in low light
- Results: Harsh shadows, blown-out windows, dark corners

**Professional approach:**
- Shoot during "golden hours" (10 AM - 2 PM for natural light)
- Use HDR (High Dynamic Range) techniques
- Balance indoor/outdoor light (windows aren't blown out)
- Supplemental lighting to eliminate dark areas

**Impact:** Spaces look bright, inviting, and accurately represented

---

**2. Correct Composition**

**Amateur mistake:** Shooting from standing eye level, cluttered frame
- Results: Boring perspective, distracting elements

**Professional approach:**
- Varied heights (kneeling for room scale, elevated for overview)
- Leading lines (draw eye into the space)
- Rule of thirds (balanced, pleasing composition)
- Decluttered frame (remove personal items, clutter)

**Impact:** Professional, magazine-quality images

---

**3. Wide-Angle Done Right**

**Amateur mistake:** Using phone wide-angle (distorts rooms)
- Results: Rooms look tiny, warped, unnatural

**Professional approach:**
- Professional wide-angle lens (16-24mm full-frame equivalent)
- Correct perspective (rooms look spacious but natural)
- Show flow between rooms
- Capture full room in one shot

**Impact:** Guests understand room scale accurately

## Common Photography Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake #1: Using Flash Indoors

**Problem:** Harsh shadows, blown-out windows, unnatural look

**Solution:** Use natural light + HDR processing

---

### Mistake #2: Shooting with Wide-Angle Phone Lens

**Problem:** Distorted rooms, tiny appearance

**Solution:** Professional wide-angle lens (16-24mm) + perspective correction

---

### Mistake #3: Including Personal Items

**Problem:** Cluttered, unprofessional, invades privacy

**Solution:** Remove all personal items before shooting

---

### Mistake #4: Not Showing All Rooms

**Problem:** Guests have unanswered questions, hesitant to book

**Solution:** Complete coverage (20-30 photos for 2-bedroom)

---

### Mistake #5: Poor Photo Order

**Problem:** Most appealing photos not shown first

**Solution:** Exterior → Living → Kitchen → Best Bedroom → Other bedrooms → Bathrooms → Amenities

---

### Mistake #6: Outdated Photos

**Problem:** Photos don't match current property (furniture changed)

**Solution:** Update photos annually or after renovations

---

### Mistake #7: Dark, Underexposed Photos

**Problem:** Spaces look uninviting, small

**Solution:** Shoot during bright daylight, use HDR to balance light

## How to Prepare for Your Photography Session

### 1 Week Before:

**Clean thoroughly:**
- Deep clean entire property
- Windows (inside and out)
- Mirrors and glass surfaces
- Floors and carpets

**Declutter:**
- Remove personal items (family photos, personal belongings)
- Clear countertops (kitchen, bathroom)
- Organize closets (neat appearance)
- Remove unnecessary furniture (if cramped)

**Repair/Replace:**
- Fix any visible damage
- Replace burned-out lightbulbs
- Touch up paint if needed
- Replace worn linens/towels

---

### 1 Day Before:

**Final preparations:**
- Fresh linens on all beds
- Fluff pillows and arrange neatly
- Set dining table (place settings for visual appeal)
- Add lifestyle touches (magazines, books, decorative items)
- Test all lights (replace any that are out)
- Clean windows again (fingerprints appear in photos)

---

- Provide access (keys, passcode, meet photographer)

- Wait in another area (2-4 hours depending on package)

- All staging and arrangement
