## How to Choose an Airbnb Management Service

### Evaluation Criteria

**Use this checklist when comparing managers:**

#### 1. Track Record

**Ask:**
- How long have you been operating?
- How many properties do you manage?
- What is your average occupancy rate?
- Can you show performance data?

**Green Flag:** 3+ years, 50+ properties, 60%+ occupancy
**Red Flag:** New operation, vague performance claims

---

#### 2. Fee Structure

**Ask:**
- What is your EXACT management fee?
- Are there ANY other fees?
- Do you markup cleaning or maintenance?
- Who pays platform fees?

**Green Flag:** All fees disclosed, no markup
**Red Flag:** Vague "plus expenses", hidden markups

---

#### 3. Services Included

**Ask:**
- What's included in your fee?
- Is photography included?
- Do you handle all platforms or just Airbnb?
- What's your response time?

**Green Flag:** Full-service, 5-platform, <1 hour response
**Red Flag:** Basic services, extra charges for essentials

---

#### 4. Contract Terms

**Ask:**
- How long is the contract?
- Can I terminate early?
- Is there a penalty for leaving?
- What happens to existing bookings if I switch?

**Green Flag:** 12 months, 30-day notice, no penalty
**Red Flag:** 24+ months, early termination fees

---

#### 5. References

**Ask:**
- Can I speak to 2-3 existing owners?
- Can I visit a managed property?
- Do you have case studies I can review?

**Green Flag:** Happy to provide references
**Red Flag:** "Privacy" excuses, no transparency

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake #1: Choosing Based on Lowest Fee

**The Trap:**
- Manager A: 15% commission
- Manager B: 25% commission
- You pick Manager A to "save money"

**Reality:**
- Manager A: 19% occupancy = RM35,000 revenue - RM5,250 fee = RM29,750
- Manager B: 35% occupancy = RM68,000 revenue - RM17,000 fee = RM51,000

**Winner:** Manager B (who charges MORE) delivers RM21,250 MORE net income.

**Lesson:** Focus on NET income, not fee percentage.

---

### Mistake #2: Not Verifying Performance

**Red Flags:**
- "We maximize your returns" (vague)
- "High occupancy guaranteed" (no numbers)
- Refusal to show actual data

**What You Want:**
- "Our average occupancy is 62%"
- "Similar units earn RM65,000-75,000 annually"
- "Here's a case study from Silverscape"

---

### Mistake #3: Ignoring Contract Terms

**Dangerous Clauses:**
- Automatic renewal (traps you for years)
- Early termination penalties (RM5,000+)
- Exclusivity without performance guarantees
- Ownership of your listings (if you leave, you lose everything)

**Safe Terms:**
- 12-month maximum
- 30-day notice to terminate
- No penalties for underperformance
- You own your listings and guest data

---

### Mistake #4: Not Testing Service Before Committing

**Do This Before Signing:**
1. WhatsApp them with a question (test response time)
2. Request to speak to existing owners
3. Ask to visit a managed property
4. Request a detailed quote (see how transparent they are)
