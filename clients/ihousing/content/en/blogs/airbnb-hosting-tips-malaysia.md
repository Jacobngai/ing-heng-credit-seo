# Airbnb Hosting Tips Malaysia: Proven Strategies for 5-Star Reviews

**Being an Airbnb host in Malaysia is rewarding. But it's also work.**

A lot of work.

Guests messaging at 11 PM. Cleaning coordination. Check-ins at midnight. "The WiFi isn't working" at 6 AM on Sunday.

**Do it right, and you'll earn RM50,000-70,000/year from a single condo.**

**Do it wrong, and you'll be begging for bookings at RM80/night, drowning in 3-star reviews.**

After managing 100+ properties in Melaka for 8 years, we've learned what works. These are our proven hosting tips for Malaysian Airbnb hosts.

## Part 1: Setup Essentials (Before Your First Guest)

### Tip #1: Invest in Quality Mattresses

**The #1 Complaint in Reviews:**

*"The bed was uncomfortable."*

*"Mattress was too hard/soft."*

*"Woke up with back pain."*

**Reality:** Guests won't return (or leave 5 stars) if they don't sleep well.

---

**What We Recommend:**

| Room Type | Mattress Type | Budget Range |
|-----------|--------------|--------------|
| **Master Bedroom** | Queen/King pocket spring | RM1,200-2,000 |
| **Second Bedroom** | Queen pocket spring | RM800-1,500 |
| **Single Beds** | Single foam/spring hybrid | RM400-800 |

**Our Go-To Brands:**
- Simmons (premium)
- Sealy (mid-high range)
- King Koil (mid-range)
- Dreamland (budget-friendly but decent)

**Rule of Thumb:** Budget RM1,000+ per mattress. It pays for itself in better reviews.

---

### Tip #2: Fast, Reliable WiFi is Non-Negotiable

**Post-Pandemic Reality:**
- 60% of guests work remotely (at least part-time)
- Kids stream YouTube/Netflix
- Guests post Instagram stories in real-time

**Slow WiFi = 1-star reviews.**

---

**What We Provide:**

| Speed | Provider | Monthly Cost |
|-------|----------|--------------|
| **100 Mbps** | Time, Unifi, Maxis | RM120-180 |
| **500 Mbps** | Time Fiber | RM200-280 |
| **Dedicated Router** | TP-Link, Asus | RM300-500 one-time |

**Setup Tips:**
- Place router centrally in unit
- Test speed in all rooms
- Provide WiFi password prominently
- Include network name AND password (don't make guests guess)

---

### Tip #3: Blackout Curtains in ALL Bedrooms

**Malaysia Sun Reality:**
- Sunrise: 7 AM
- Guests want to sleep until 9-10 AM
- Thin curtains = wake up at 7 AM

**Review Impact:**

*"Blackout curtains were amazing - slept until 10 AM!"* (5 stars)

*"No blackout curtains, woke up with the sun."* (3 stars)

---

**What to Buy:**
- Ready-made blackout curtains (IKEA, MR.DIY)
- Custom curtains (budget RM400-800 per window)
- Eye masks (backup, budget RM10/piece)

**Result:** Better sleep = better reviews.

---

### Tip #4: Air Conditioning in All Rooms

**Malaysia Climate Reality:**
- Hot and humid year-round
- Guests expect AC
- No AC in bedrooms = booking cancellations

**Minimum Setup:**
- 1 AC in living area
- 1 AC in each bedroom

**Our Recommendation:**
- Inverter AC (more energy efficient)
- 1 HP per 150-200 sqft
- Remote control accessible from bed

**Cost:** RM2,000-4,000 per unit (installed)

**ROI:** Guests won't book without it.

---

**Our Setup:**

- Netflix subscription (RM55/month)

**Cost:**

**Dynamic Pricing Solution:**

**Example Pricing (2-bedroom town centre):**

| Period | Rate | Strategy |
|--------|------|----------|
| **Low Season Weekday** | RM150-170 | Attract budget travelers |
| **Low Season Weekend** | RM190-220 | Weekend demand |
| **High Season Weekday** | RM190-220 | Moderate demand |
| **High Season Weekend** | RM240-280 | Peak pricing |
| **Peak Events** | RM280-350 | Maximum revenue |

**Result:** +25-35% revenue vs. fixed pricing

## Part 3: Guest Communication

### Tip #11: Fast Response Time is Critical

**Airbnb's Superhost Requirement:** Respond to 90% of messages within 24 hours.

**Our Standard:** Respond within 1 hour during business hours, 4 hours maximum.

**Why Speed Matters:**
- Guests book multiple properties
- First to respond often wins the booking
- Last-minute bookings are time-sensitive

---

**Templates That Work:**

**Initial Inquiry Response:**
*"Hi [Guest Name], thanks for your inquiry! Yes, [Property Name] is available for your dates. We'd love to host you. Let me know if you have any questions, or feel free to book directly. Best regards, [Your Name]"*

**Booking Confirmation:**
*"Great news! Your booking is confirmed. Check-in is from 3 PM, checkout by 11 AM. I'll send detailed check-in instructions and WiFi password 2 days before arrival. Looking forward to hosting you!"*

**Pre-Arrival (2 days before):**
*"Hi [Guest Name], excited for your upcoming stay! Here's your check-in info: [Instructions]. WiFi: [Network] / [Password]. If you need anything during your stay, just message. Safe travels!"*

---

### Tip #12: Clear Check-In Instructions

**Avoid Confusion:**

**Bad Instructions:**
*"Key is under the mat. Unit is on 5th floor."*

**Good Instructions:**
*"CHECK-IN INSTRUCTIONS:
1. Drive to [Address] - parking is available on Level 2
2. Take Lift B to 5th floor
3. Unit number is 5-12 (turn left from lift)
4. Key lock code: 1234#
5. WiFi: MyNetwork / Password123
Need help? Call/WhatsApp: +60123456789"*

**Visual Aids:**
- Photos of building entrance
- Photo of lift lobby
- Photo of unit door

---

### Tip #13: Welcome Book

**Physical or Digital Welcome Book Should Include:**

**Essential Information:**
- WiFi network and password
- AC remotes (how to use)
- Hot water (how to switch on)
- Emergency contact numbers
- Checkout instructions

**Local Tips:**
- Recommended restaurants (with walking directions)
- Nearby attractions
- Grab/food delivery info
- Local emergency numbers

**House Rules:**
- No smoking
- No parties
- Quiet hours (10 PM - 8 AM)
- Trash disposal

## Part 4: During the Stay

### Tip #14: Be Available But Not Intrusive

**Good Balance:**

**DO:**
- Respond quickly to messages
- Proactively check in after arrival
- Offer local recommendations
- Address issues immediately

**DON'T:**
- Visit the property unannounced
- Message excessively
- Nag about house rules (be polite)
- Overstay welcome

**Template:**
*"Hi [Guest Name], hope you're enjoying your stay! Just checking in - is everything okay with the unit? Let me know if you need anything or have questions. Enjoy Melaka!"*

---

### Tip #15: Handle Issues Gracefully

**Something Will Go Wrong. Eventually.**

**AC breaks. WiFi stops. Toilet clogs.**

**How You Handle It = Review Score**

---

**Bad Response:**
*"It's not my problem. Call maintenance yourself."* → 1 star

**Good Response:**
*"So sorry about the AC! I'm contacting maintenance now. They'll be there within 2 hours. In the meantime, I've set up a fan in each room. Can I offer you a partial refund for tonight?"* → 5 stars (if resolved quickly)

**Script for Issues:**
1. **Acknowledge and apologize**
2. **Take ownership** (even if not your fault)
3. **Provide immediate solution**
4. **Offer compensation** (if appropriate)
5. **Follow up** to ensure resolved

### Tip #19: Request Reviews Strategically

**When to Ask:**
- Immediately after checkout (while excitement is fresh)
- After positive guest message
- After resolving any issues successfully

**How to Ask:**
*"Thanks for staying with us! We loved hosting you. If you enjoyed your stay, would you mind leaving us a review? It really helps other guests and helps us improve. Hope to host you again!"*

**Never:**
- Beg for reviews
- Offer incentives for reviews (violates Airbnb policy)
- Only ask 5-star guests (unfair)
- Send multiple reminders (annoying)

---

### Tip #20: Handle Negative Reviews Professionally

**Response Framework:**

**Don't:**
- Argue publicly
- Blame the guest
- Get defensive
- Ignore it

**Do:**
- Acknowledge the issue
- Apologize sincerely
- Explain what you've done to fix it
- Take it offline ("Please message us directly")

---

**Template Responses:**

**For Cleanliness Complaint:**
*"Hi [Guest Name], I'm so sorry your stay didn't meet cleanliness standards. This is not our usual standard. I've addressed this with our cleaning team and they've re-cleaned the unit. I'd love to make it right - please message me directly so we can discuss."*

**For Noise Complaint:**
*"Hi [Guest Name], I apologize for the noise disturbance. We take guest comfort seriously. I've reminded all neighbors about quiet hours and provided earplugs for future guests. Thank you for bringing this to my attention."*

**For Amenities Not Working:**
*"Hi [Guest Name], I'm sorry [item] wasn't working during your stay. We've now [fixed/replaced] it and added it to our pre-arrival checklist to prevent future issues. I'd like to offer a partial refund for this inconvenience."*

### Tip #22: Minimum Stay Optimization

**The Balance:**

**1-Night Minimums:**
- Pros: More bookings
- Cons: More turnover, more cleaning, more work

**3-Night Minimums:**
- Pros: Less work, less wear
- Cons: Fewer bookings, empty midweek

**Our Strategy:**
- 1-night minimum (flexibility)
- 2-night minimum on weekends (discourage 1-night weekend stays)
- 3-night minimum on peak holidays (discourage short stays during high demand)

**Result:** Optimize occupancy vs. workload.

---

### Tip #23: Instant Book vs. Request to Book

**Instant Book:**
- Pros: More bookings (guests love instant confirmation)
- Cons: Less control over who books

**Request to Book:**
- Pros: Screen guests
- Cons: Lose bookings (guests don't want to wait)

**Our Recommendation:**
- Enable Instant Book
- Set strict house rules
- Require government ID verification
- Allow 4-hour window to decline (if needed)

**Result:** More bookings, minimal risk.

## Part 8: Legal and Regulatory

### Tip #24: MOTAC Compliance (2024+)

**New Requirement:** All Malaysian short-term rentals must register with MOTAC.

**What's Required:**
1. Property registration
2. Basic safety standards:
   - Fire extinguisher
   - Smoke detector
   - First aid kit
   - Emergency contact display
3. Guest information display

**Cost:** Minimal (administrative)
**Enforcement:** Ongoing (2024-2025)

**Our Status:** All managed properties compliant.
