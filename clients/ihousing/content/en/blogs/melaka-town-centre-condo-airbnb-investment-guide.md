## Heritage Zone Restrictions: What You Need to Know

### Is Airbnb Legal in Melaka's Heritage Zone?

**Short Answer:** Yes, but with restrictions.

**Longer Answer:** Melaka's UNESCO World Heritage Site has special regulations aimed at preserving the area's character.

### Key Restrictions

**1. Building Type Restrictions**

**Allowed:**
- Condominiums (strata-titled)
- Apartments in mixed developments
- Commercial properties with proper licensing

**Restricted/Prohibited:**
- Traditional shophouses (residential zone) - check local council
- Heritage buildings (may require special permits)
- Certain residential zones - verify with MPMB (Melaka Historic City Council)

**2. Signage & Display**

**Rules:**
- No exterior signage advertising short-term rental
- No commercial displays visible from street
- Business must operate discreetly

**What We Do:**
- List units online (Airbnb, Booking.com) only
- No physical signage at property
- Low-profile, respectful operation

**3. Guest Behavior Expectations**

**Heritage Zone Considerations:**
- Quiet hours strictly enforced (respect neighbors)
- No parties or gatherings
- Parking limitations (some areas have restricted parking)
- Waste disposal rules (strict in heritage zone)

**Enforcement:**
- Neighbors can complain to MPMB
- Fines possible for violations
- Repeat offenses = potential shutdown

**4. MOTAC Licensing (2024 Requirement)**

**As of 2024:** All Malaysian Airbnb properties must register with MOTAC (Ministry of Tourism, Arts and Culture).

**Requirements:**
- Property registration
- Basic safety standards
- Fire extinguisher + smoke detector
- Guest information display

**Cost:** Minimal (registration-based)
**Impact:** Formalizes short-term rental industry

### Challenge #2: Strata Rules

**Condo associations often restrict Airbnb:**

**Common Restrictions:**
- Minimum rental period (some require 3+ months)
- Quota on short-term rentals (cap on number of units)
- Additional fees for commercial use

- Parking restrictions

**How We Navigate:**
- Review bylaws before purchase (we help investors assess)

## Legal & Regulatory Considerations

### MOTAC Licensing (2024)

**New Requirement:** All short-term rental properties must register with Ministry of Tourism.

**What's Required:**
1. Property registration
2. Basic safety standards:
   - Fire extinguisher
   - Smoke detector
   - First aid kit
   - Emergency contact display
3. Guest information display

**Cost:** Minimal (administrative)
**Enforcement:** Ongoing (2024-2025)

**Our Status:** All managed properties are compliant / in process of compliance.

---

### MPMB Regulations

**Melaka Historic City Council** oversees the heritage zone.

**Current Stance:**
- No outright ban on Airbnb
- Enforcement focuses on:
  - Nuisance complaints (noise, parties)
  - Unauthorized signage
  - Business operations in prohibited zones

**Best Practice:**
- Fly under the radar (low profile)
- Maintain good neighbor relations
- Respond quickly to complaints
- No exterior signage

---

### Income Tax

**Airbnb income is taxable:**

**For Individuals:**
- Declare as rental income
- Deductible expenses:
  - Mortgage interest
  - Maintenance
  - Utilities
  - Management fees
  - Platform fees

**Tax Rate:** Progressive (0-28% based on total income)

**Recommendation:** Consult tax advisor for specific situation.
