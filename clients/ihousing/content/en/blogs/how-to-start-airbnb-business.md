## Step 3: Secure Legal Compliance

Don't skip this. Operating illegally risks fines and shutdown.

### MOTAC License (Required)

**What it is:**
Ministry of Tourism, Arts and Culture license for accommodation businesses.

**Who needs it:**
All short-term rental operators in Malaysia.

**How to apply:**
1. Register business with SSM (Suruhanjaya Syarikat Malaysia)
2. Apply via MOTAC online portal
3. Submit documents:
   - SSM business registration
   - Property ownership proof
   - Fire certificate (if required)
   - Local council approval

**Cost:** RM150-500 registration + RM100-300 annual renewal
**Timeline:** 2-6 weeks

### Local Council Approval

**Required for:**
- Signage (if displaying)
- Change of use (if commercial)
- Parking allocation

### Strata Compliance

**CRITICAL:** Verify your building allows short-term rental before buying.

**Check:**
✅ Strata title rules on commercial use
✅ Guest visitor policies
✅ Parking allocation for guests
✅ Any owner-occupancy restrictions

### Tax Registration

**Register with LHDN:**
- Business income is taxable
- Expenses are deductible
- Annual filing required

## Step 6: Create Your Listing

Your listing is your storefront. Make it count.

### Professional Photography (Non-Negotiable)

**Why it matters:**
- Listings with pro photos get 30-40% more bookings
- Better photos justify higher rates
- First impressions happen in seconds

**Cost:** RM300-800
**ROI:** Pays for itself in the first month

### Writing Your Listing

**Compelling Title:**
- Include key features
- Mention location benefits
- Keep it under 50 characters

Examples:
- "Modern 2BR @ UNESCO Heritage Site"
- "Beachfront Condo with Pool View"
- "City Centre 2BR near Jonker Street"

**Description Structure:**
1. Hook (why choose this property)
2. Space (what guests get)
3. Location (what's nearby)
4. Guest access (facilities)
5. Other things to note (house rules)
6. Host interaction

**Amenities:**
- List everything accurately
- Highlight unique features
- Be specific (e.g., "High-speed WiFi 100Mbps")

## Step 7: Set Your Pricing Strategy

Pricing is both art and science. Get it wrong and you leave money on the table or kill occupancy.

### Pricing Tiers

**Base Rate:** Standard nightly rate for normal demand
- 2-bedroom city centre: RM120-160
- 2-bedroom beachfront: RM160-220
- Studio/1-bedroom: RM80-130

**Dynamic Adjustments:**
- Weekends: +30-50%
- Holidays: +50-100%
- School holidays: +40-60%
- Events: +75-150%

**Low-Demand Pricing:**
- Weekdays: -15-25%
- Off-peak periods: -15-20%
- Last-minute: -15% (within 48 hours)

### Pricing Psychology

**Charm pricing:** RM149 instead of RM150
**Weekly discount:** 10% off for 7+ nights
**Monthly discount:** 20% off for 28+ nights
**Early bird:** 5% off for 30+ days advance booking

**Pro tip:** Use dynamic pricing tools or hire managers who do. Fixed pricing loses money.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Under-Furnishing

**Problem:** Cheap furniture shows in photos and disappoints guests.

**Solution:** Budget RM20,000-35,000 for proper furnishing. ROI is rapid.

### Mistake 2: DIY Photography

**Problem:** Phone photos look amateur and get 30-40% fewer bookings.

**Solution:** Spend RM300-800 on professional photography. Immediate ROI.

### Mistake 3: Fixed Pricing

**Problem:** Same rate every day leaves money on the table.

**Solution:** Use dynamic pricing or hire managers who do.

### Mistake 4: Ignoring Reviews

**Problem:** Bad reviews or review drought kills future bookings.

**Solution:** Provide excellent experience and actively request reviews.

### Mistake 5: Going It Alone

**Problem:** Self-management often leads to lower net income.

**Solution:** Start with professional management or transition after burnout hits.
