## Understanding Melaka's Regulatory Status (2025)

### Current Legal Status

As of January 2025:

1. **No State-Level Ban**: Melaka state government has not enacted laws prohibiting short-term rentals
2. **No Licensing Required**: Unlike hotels, Airbnb condos don't need tourism licenses (yet)
3. **No Minimum Stay**: Unlike Selangor, you can accept 1-night bookings
4. **No Income Cap**: Earn as much as your property can generate

### What's Coming? (Future Regulations)

The Ministry of Tourism, Arts and Culture (MOTAC) has **discussed** requiring STR licenses, but:

- **Not yet implemented** as of 2025
- Would apply nationwide, not Melaka-specific
- Likely to focus on **registration** rather than prohibition
- Grandfathering expected for existing hosts

**Recommendation:** Start now while regulations are still friendly. Future hosts may face stricter requirements.

## Condo vs Landed Property: Different Rules

### Strata-Titled Condos (Apartments, Service Residences)

**The Challenge:** Each condo's management corporation sets its own rules.

**Common Condo Restrictions:**
- No commercial activity (Airbnb may fall under this)
- Minimum rental period (30 days, 6 months, etc.)
- Guest registration requirements
- Parking limitations

**Before Buying:** Always check with the building management if Airbnb is allowed. We can help verify this for condos we manage.

### Landed Properties (Terrace Houses, Bungalows)

**Easier Rules:** Landed properties typically have fewer restrictions:
- No strata management to deal with
- No condo committee approval needed
- More freedom to operate

**Trade-off:** Lower occupancy compared to prime condo locations near Jonker Street.

## Compliance Checklist (What You SHOULD Do)

Even without strict regulations, follow these best practices:

### 1. Inform Your Condo Management
- Transparency avoids conflicts
- Some condos require written notification
- Provide emergency contact information

### 2. Register with Tourism Malaysia (Optional)
- Not mandatory yet but shows professionalism
- May become required in future
- Displays commitment to quality standards

### 3. Have House Rules
- Quiet hours (e.g., 10 PM - 7 AM)
- No smoking inside
- No parties/events
- Maximum guest limits
- Parking instructions

### 4. Get Insurance
- Fire insurance
- Public liability insurance
- Host protection insurance (Airbnb offers this)

### 5. Track Income for Tax
- Declare rental income to LHDN
- Deduct expenses (furniture, utilities, management fees)
- Keep proper receipts
