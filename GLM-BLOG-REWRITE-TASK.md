## Writing Style

### DO:
- Write conversationally but professionally
- Use short paragraphs (2-3 sentences)
- Address reader as "you"
- Reference Malaysian context (RM, local regulations)
- Include specific equipment examples
- Explain WHY something matters, not just what
- Use transition sentences between sections
- Make it scannable with headers

### DON'T:
- ❌ NO specific approval times ("2 hours", "24 hours", "same day")
- ❌ NO specific interest rates
- ❌ NO competitor names
- ❌ NO guaranteed approval promises
- ❌ NO fluff or filler content
- ❌ NO repetitive bullet lists without explanation

```
Why Equipment Financing Makes Sense for Malaysian SMEs

If you're running a construction business or logistics operation, you know that cash flow is everything. One broken excavator or the need for an additional lorry can mean the difference between winning a contract and watching it go to your competitor.

Traditional options - draining your savings or hoping the bank approves your loan - often leave business owners stuck. That's where equipment financing offers a smarter path forward.

Unlike depleting your working capital on a single purchase, equipment financing lets you spread the cost over time while putting the equipment to work immediately. Your excavator or forklift starts generating revenue from day one, helping pay for itself.

For Malaysian SMEs especially, this approach preserves the cash buffer you need for payroll, fuel, maintenance, and unexpected expenses. Instead of risking your business stability on a large purchase, you maintain financial flexibility while still growing your capabilities.
```
